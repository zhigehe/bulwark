Information security company [CyberArk](https://www.cyberark.com/) has [acquired](https://www.cyberark.com/press/cyberark-acquires-zilla-security-to-reshape-identity-governance-and-administration-for-the-modern-enterprise/) identity access and governance platform [Zilla Security](https://zillasecurity.com/) in a deal worth up to $175 million.

Founded in 1999, Boston-based CyberArk specializes in [access management](https://www.cyberark.com/products/access-management/), including [privileged access security](https://www.cyberark.com/products/privileged-access-manager/) which helps organizations protect sensitive data and critical infrastructure from external (and internal) threats.

CyberArk went public on the Nasdaq [in 2014](https://www.cyberark.com/press/cyberark-announces-pricing-initial-public-offering/), with its shares [surging 41% in the past year to an all-time high](https://uk.investing.com/news/company-news/cyberark-stock-soars-to-alltime-high-of-36435-amid-growth-surge-93CH-3889946) valuation of more than $18 billion. Earlier today, the company posted its [Q4 results](https://investors.cyberark.com/news/news-details/2025/CyberArk-Announces-Record-Fourth-Quarter-and-Full-Year-2024-Results/default.aspx), reporting a revenue hike of 41%, with its subscription revenue in particular rising 62%. Moreover, its annual recurring revenue (ARR) surpassed the $1 billion mark.
