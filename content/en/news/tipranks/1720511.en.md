The job cuts are the latest cost-cutting measure to be implemented by the social media company behind Facebook and Instagram. Since 2022, Meta Platforms has cut about 20% of its worldwide staff, resulting in tens of thousands of people being pink slipped.

In a memo to staff, Meta said it is targeting low performers in the latest workforce reduction. The planned cuts will affect 5% of [Meta’s employees](https://www.tipranks.com/news/meta-earnings-meta-platforms-financial-results-top-wall-street-forecasts), or about 3,600 people. In the staff memo, Meta said employees who are laid off will be cut off from the company’s internal technology system within an hour and will be notified by email of their severance package.

**Shifting Resources**
----------------------

Meta added that it has no plans to send out any internal communications about the staff layoffs after they are undertaken in the coming days. Meta Platforms is one of several technology companies that is cutting staff this year as they [shift resources](https://www.tipranks.com/news/meta-platforms-meta-to-spend-100-billion-on-virtual-reality-smart-glasses) to investments in [artificial intelligence](https://www.tipranks.com/compare-stocks/artificial-intelligence) (AI).

Amazon [(AMZN)](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/amzn), Microsoft [(MSFT)](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/msft), and Salesforce [(CRM)](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/crm) have also announced job cuts so far in 2025. The shift coincides with an increase in [AI](https://www.tipranks.com/compare-stocks/artificial-intelligence) spending, including at Meta Platforms which has announced plans to [spend $65 billion](https://www.tipranks.com/news/meta-platforms-meta-to-invest-65b-to-build-out-its-ai-infrastructure) to buildout its AI infrastructure.

[META stock has risen 53%](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/meta) over the last 12 months.
