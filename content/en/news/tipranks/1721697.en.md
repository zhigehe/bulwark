Ilya Sutskever, former chief scientist at OpenAI, is on a mission to create a new vision and future for [AI](https://www.tipranks.com/compare-stocks/artificial-intelligence) that surpasses human intelligence and aligns with human values and goals. His latest initiative, Safe Superintelligence (SSI), an AI startup SSI, is tackling some of the most challenging issues in AI, ensuring that advanced systems are safe and beneficial for humanity. With offices in Palo Alto and Tel Aviv, the company has garnered plenty of attention thanks to Sutskever’s celebrated reputation in generative AI.

According to a recent *Reuters* report, SSI is now looking to raise funds in its latest round, aiming for a valuation of at least $20 billion. This is a substantial increase from its last funding round in September 2024, when it raised $1 billion at a $5 billion valuation. The new round, still in early discussions, seeks to attract existing and new investors. While the amount of capital being raised is unclear, the talks highlight growing confidence in SSI’s vision and the potential for exponential growth in the AI sector.

**What Is SSI?**
----------------

In short, SSI is a newly founded AI research company focused on developing superintelligence—AI systems that surpass human intelligence—while ensuring its safety and alignment with human values. Unlike other AI labs that balance commercialization with safety, SSI is solely dedicated to building superintelligence in a controlled, secure manner.

SSI’s ambitious goals and non-profit approach to developing safe AI, which has promised to “scale in peace” by focusing on long-term goals rather than immediate commercial pressures, may appeal to investors looking for sustainability over quick returns. SSI’s commitment to advancing AI responsibly could set it apart from other AI labs in an industry where short-term financial gains often overshadow research-driven growth. However, investors should also consider the changing dynamics within the AI landscape.

The rise of low-cost alternatives, such as DeepSeek, a Chinese startup developing competitive AI models at a fraction of the cost, may influence market conditions and investor sentiment. Despite these disruptions, SSI’s unique approach and Sutskever’s backing make it intriguing to follow, and this latest round of fundraising could signal the path forward for the new AI venture.
