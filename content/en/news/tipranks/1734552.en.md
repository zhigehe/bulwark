For its Fiscal second quarter, Cisco reported earnings per share (EPS) of $0.94, which came in ahead of [consensus estimates](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/csco/earnings) of $0.91. Revenue in the quarter of $13.99 billion beat the $13.87 billion that was forecast among analysts. The company’s sales were up 9% from a year earlier.

The latest results are a reversal for Cisco after the company’s revenue declined in each of the four previous quarters. Management attributed the positive results to orders for [artificial intelligence](https://www.tipranks.com/compare-stocks/artificial-intelligence) (AI) infrastructure that exceeded $350 million during the period.

**Look Ahead**
--------------

In addition to its strong print, [Cisco Systems](https://www.tipranks.com/news/is-cisco-csco-the-superior-investment-compared-to-palo-alto-networks-panw) offered bullish forward guidance, saying it now expects earnings per share (EPS) of $3.68 to $3.74 for the 2025 Fiscal year, with $56 billion to $56.50 billion in revenue. Analysts had been looking for $3.66 in earnings and $55.99 billion in sales from the company.

Cisco also highlighted that revenue from its networking division totaled $6.85 billion, greater than the $6.67 billion consensus estimate among analysts who cover the company. Additionally Cisco’s security unit contributed $2.11 billion to the latest quarterly results, a 117% increase from a year earlier thanks to the addition of Splunk, which it acquired in March 2024 for $27 billion.

[CSCO stock has risen 30%](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/csco) over the last year.

**Is CSCO Stock a Buy?**
------------------------

The stock of Cisco Systems has a consensus Moderate Buy rating among 16 Wall Street analysts. That rating is based on nine Buy and seven Hold recommendations issue in the last three months. The [average CSCO price target](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/csco/forecast) of $63.67 implies 1.82% upside from current levels. These ratings are likely to change after these latest financial results.
