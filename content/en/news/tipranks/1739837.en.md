Shares of Roku [(ROKU)](https://www.tipranks.com/stocks/roku) surged in after-hours trading after the streaming company reported earnings for its fourth quarter of Fiscal Year 2024. Earnings per share came in at -$0.24, which beat analysts’ consensus estimate of -$0.41 per share. In addition, sales increased by 21.9% year-over-year, with revenue hitting $1.2 billion. This beat analysts’ expectations by $50 million.

When breaking down the numbers further, Roku reported impressive results for its Platform segment, with revenue surpassing $1 billion for the first time and growing 25% year-over-year. The company also expanded its reach in the U.S., as its services are now available in over half of broadband households.

Furthermore, the Roku Channel saw significant growth, with streaming hours increasing by 82% year-over-year in Q4. The channel is now available in 89.8 million U.S. households, which is an increase of 9.8 million from 2023. Roku is also making progress in growing ad demand through partnerships with third-party platforms and by improving the overall user experience. As a result, Average Revenue Per User grew by 4% year-over-year to $41.49.

**Roku’s Outlook for 2025**
---------------------------

For the first quarter of 2025, Roku expects total net revenue to reach $1.005 billion, which is in line with estimates. At the same time, Platform revenue is anticipated to grow by 16% year-over-year, while device revenue is expected to be largely flat due to elevated inventory levels. The company also expects a total gross profit of $450 million and adjusted EBITDA of $55 million for the quarter.

For the full year 2025, Roku anticipates total net revenue of $4.610 billion, which is slightly below estimates of $4.617 billion, with platform revenue growing 12% year-over-year to $3.950 billion. In addition, Device revenue is expected to grow 12% to reach $660 million. Furthermore, the company now expects to see positive operating income by 2026.
