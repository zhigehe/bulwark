It seems that attention is turning away from the fleeting allure of memecoins towards the foundational solidity of Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other Layer-1 cryptocurrencies. According to the latest data from Santiment, top Layer-1 blockchains now dominate a hefty 44.2% of [crypto](https://www.tipranks.com/cryptocurrency) discussions. This has dwarfed the chatter around the top six memecoins, which stands at just 4%.

**A More Mature Market Emerges**
--------------------------------

This shift in focus suggests a maturing market, moving away from speculative quick wins towards assets with real-world applications and more substantial backing. “Increased focus on these assets usually reflects a more mature and informed approach by the community, which prioritizes security, innovation, and real-world adoption,” notes the Santiment report. This could be a sign of a healthier, more stable market cycle taking root. In fact, this could offer a more sustainable environment for growth and innovation in the crypto space.

**Technical Indicators Signal Market Shifts**
---------------------------------------------

Meanwhile, [huge amounts of](https://www.tipranks.com/news/ethereum-could-blast-toward-3k-on-rocket-like-rebound) [Ethereum](https://www.tipranks.com/cryptocurrency/eth-usd) holdings are changing hands, with Santiment reporting that over 224,410 Ether left exchanges in a single day. This marked the most considerable movement in two years. This trend is seen as a positive sign for Ethereum’s price stability and investor confidence. On the flip side, the [Bitcoin](https://www.tipranks.com/cryptocurrency/btc-usd) market observed movement of 14,000 BTC that had been dormant for 7-10 years, though these coins have not hit the exchanges for sale. This typically indicates that they are not poised for immediate liquidation.

**Federal Reserve’s Stance Influences Crypto Dynamics**
-------------------------------------------------------

It’s important to consider the broader economic backdrop, especially as recent comments from Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell hinted at a cautious approach to interest rate cuts. Powell’s remarks at the Senate Banking Committee emphasized that the U.S. economy remains robust, suggesting no rush to adjust rates. This scenario typically [bodes well for riskier assets like cryptocurrencies](https://www.tipranks.com/news/bitcoin-braces-for-impact-as-u-s-cpi-report-could-stir-the-crypto-waters), which thrive in low-interest-rate environments.

Crypto analysts are divided on the implications of the Federal Reserve’s current stance for a potential “altcoin season.” While some see the lack of immediate quantitative easing (QE) as a negative, others believe that moderate economic expansion and credit growth could sustain a risk-on sentiment in the markets.
