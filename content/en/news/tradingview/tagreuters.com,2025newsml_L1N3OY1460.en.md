Basis bids for corn and soybeans shipped by barge to the U.S. Gulf Coast turned lower on Friday, while export premiums ticked higher on buyer interest and tighter supplies in some areas, traders said.

\* Chicago Board of Trade corn, soybean and wheat futures weakened on Friday on renewed worries that trade disputes could hurt demand for U.S. farm products, analysts said.

\* U.S. President Donald Trump said he will announce reciprocal tariffs on many countries next week, confirming a report by Reuters. Grain traders worry the duties may spark retaliation from importers that would dent U.S. crop sales.

\* CIF Gulf soybean barges loaded in March traded at 77 cents over CBOT March (SH25) futures on Thursday. Meanwhile, barges loaded in May traded at 63 cents over CBOT May soybean (SK25) futures.

\* CIF Gulf soybean barges loaded in February were bid 2 cents lower than Thursday at 68 cents over CBOT March (SH25) futures.

\* FOB export premiums for February soybean loadings were offered up 1 cent at 85 cents over March futures.

\* CIF February corn barge bids were bid 2 cents lower at 73 cents over CBOT March (CH25) futures, and February corn export premiums were offered 3 cents higher at 83 cents over futures.
