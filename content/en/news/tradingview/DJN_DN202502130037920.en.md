The U.K. can't expect a major pickup in growth in the coming months, says Deloitte's Debapratim De. The British economy unexpectedly grew in the fourth quarter, but only by 0.1%, and "underlying momentum remains weak," De writes in a note. "We continue to expect sluggish growth through spring, with the fiscal easing from last year's Autumn Budget driving a pickup in activity over summer," he says.
