AlphaValue/Baader Europe lowered its estimates for Porsche AG after the luxury carmaker said its profitability campaign includes an 800 million-euro restructuring cost.

The German stock was kept at reduce with the price target lowered to 54.7 euros from 59.6 euros. Analysts also said Feb. 7 it remains bearish on the company and expects a "significantly weaker" new year for the group.

For 2025, the research firm lowered its EPS forecast for Porsche by 26.3% 5o 3.02 euros, with the EBIT and free cash flow estimates also down. On the other hand, analysts expect sales to reach 38.7 billion euros, up slightly from its prior call but lower than the company's guidance range of 39 billion euros to 40 billion euros.
