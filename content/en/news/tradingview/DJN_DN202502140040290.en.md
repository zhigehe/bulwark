Malaysia's benchmark Kuala Lumpur Composite Index closed flat at 1591.60. The local market stayed in consolidation mode due to limited domestic catalysts and ongoing uncertainty over U.S. tariff policies, TA Securities analyst Stephen Soo said in a note. Soo pegs the KLCI's support at 1550 and resistance at 1605. Among decliners, CelcomDigi lost 8.5% as analysts raised concerns about its outlook. 99 Speed Mart Retail was 2.7% lower. Meanwhile, Genting Malaysia added 6.2% and Sunway Bhd. rose 1.7%. (yingxian.wong@wsj.com)
