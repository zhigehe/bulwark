Rakuten Bank's profit attributable to owners of the parent surged more than 40% to 35.2 billion yen in the nine months ended Dec. 31,Â 2024, from 25.1 billion yen a year ago driven by a robust interest income growth.

Japan's largest online bank's basic earnings per share rose to 201.63 yen from 144.75 yen a year ago, according to a Tokyo Stock Exchange filing on Wednesday.

Ordinary income increased more than 30% to 131.7 billion yen from 101.1 billion yen a year earlier.

In a separate filing, Rakuten Bank raised its earnings forecast after recent performance exceeded expectations.

It forecasted attributable profit to grow 42% to 48.9 billion yen or 280.25 yen per share (from 46.2 billion yen or 265.03 yen per share) and ordinary income to gain over 33% to 183.6 billion yen (From 178.6 billion yen) for the fiscal year ending March 31.

Rakuten Bank did not declare or forecast any dividend payment for the current year.

Shares of Rakuten Bank soared 14% in late-morning trading Thursday.
