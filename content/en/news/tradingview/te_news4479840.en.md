Bulgaria’s annual inflation rate accelerated to 3.7% in January 2025, the highest in a year, up from 2.2% in the previous month.

Main upward pressure came from prices of food & non-alcoholic beverage (4.4% vs 2.8% in December), housing & utilities (5% vs 2.8%).

Prices also increased for alcoholic beverages & tobacco (7.2% vs 6%), health (2.4% vs 1.8%), recreation & culture (5.3% vs 3.7%), restaurant & hotels (9.5% vs 7.5%), and miscellaneous goods & services (4% vs 3.4%).

At the same time, transport costs rebounded (3% vs -0.6%).

On a monthly basis, consumer prices climbed 2% in January, its highest since April 2022, following a 0.4% rise in the prior month.
