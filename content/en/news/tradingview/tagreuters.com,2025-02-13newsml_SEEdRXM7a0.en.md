ZAGREB (Croatia), February 13 (SeeNews) - The main share indices of the Zagreb Stock Exchange rose on Thursday, for a second day, as gains in hotel owner and manager Valamar [ZSE:RIVP] and Hrvatski Telekom [ZSE:HT] offset losses in electrical concern Koncar [ZSE:KOEI], bourse data showed.

The bourse’s 19-share equity benchmark, the CROBEX, added 0.08% to 3,459.45 points.

The narrower blue-chip CROBEX10 index climbed 0.05% to 2,178.65 points.

In regular trading, index heavyweight Koncar was the most traded company with a turnover of 534,000 euro ($558,000). It was also the biggest daily decliner among the blue chips as its shared shed 3.85% to 500 euro. In the last one year, they jumper 127%.

Valamar followed with a turnover of 191,000 euro, as its shares rose 0.90% to 6.74 euro on Thursday. In the last one month, they gained 28.63%.

Shares of another index heavyweight, Hrvatski Telekom, rose 1.86% to 43.80 euro in a daily turnover of 173,000 euro.

The bourse's regular turnover fell to 1.9 million euro from 2.5 million euro on Wednesday.

Besides, in a block deal of 646,000 euro a total of 10,000 preferred shares of diversified holding company Adris [ZSE:ADRS2] changed hands at a price of 64.60 euro per share.

($ = 0.958 euro)
