\*\* Shares of SBI Cards and Payment Services Ltd rise 5.7% to 862.5 rupees, highest since May 2023

\*\* Macquarie upgrades to 'outperform' from 'neutral', saying "SBI Cards is a play on recovery in the unsecured loan cycle"

\*\* India's unsecured loan segment has seen rising slippages and defaults, raising asset quality concerns

\*\* Macquarie, though, says its checks show slippages are plateauing and it expects credit costs for SBIC to start "declining materially" in next 2 quarters

\*\* It also notes other catalysts, including falling rates, easing liquidity, income tax cuts and central bank's softer stance on unsecured loans

\*\* It raises PT on SBIC to 1,000 rupees from 735 rupees

\*\* Average rating of 24 snalysts is "hold" and median PT avg PT is 785 rupees -LSEG
