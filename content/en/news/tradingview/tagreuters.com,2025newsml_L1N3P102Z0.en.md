Chinese renewable energy stocks fell as much as 3.7% after the market open on Monday, following an announcement by state planner the National Development and Reform Commission on the scaling back of subsidies for renewable power producers.

Shares of Chinese solar producer Tongwei slumped 3.78% as of 0248 GMT, while Longi Green Energy , the world's largest solar manufacturer dropped 2.03%.

Peers Jinko Solar and JA Solar were also down around 2%, and shares of key wind turbine manufacturer MingYang fell 1.26%.

By contrast, the Shanghai Composite Index was up 0.32%.
