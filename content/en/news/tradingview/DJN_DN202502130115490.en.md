Hewlett Packard Enterprise has shipped its first Nvidia Blackwell family-based technology, the GB200 NVL72, a rack-scale system aimed at service providers and large enterprises for deploying AI clusters. This system uses advanced direct liquid cooling for efficiency and performance, featuring shared-memory, low-latency architecture for AI models over a trillion parameters. It blends Nvidia CPUs, GPUs, networking, and software for handling parallel workloads like GenAI. The GB200 NVL72 includes 72 GPUs, 36 CPUs, and up to 13.5 TB of HBM3e memory with 576 TB/sec bandwidth. (chris.wack@wsj.com)
