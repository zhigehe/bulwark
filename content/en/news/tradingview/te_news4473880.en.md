The annual inflation rate in Senegal accelerated to 1.8% in January 2025 from 0.8% in the prior month, marking the highest inflation rate since last May.

The latest reading also marked the second straight month of inflation, mainly boosted by rises in prices of food and non-alcoholic beverages (3.0%), housing (1.1%), and transport (0.9%).

On a monthly basis, consumer prices rose by 1.1%, the first increase in three months, after falling by 0.8% in December and marking the fastest pace since August 2023.
