ZAGREB (Croatia), February 10 (SeeNews) - The main share indices of the Zagreb Stock Exchange gained ground backed by gains in most of the blue chips, and despite a drop of turnover, bourse data showed on Monday.

The bourse’s 19-share equity benchmark, the CROBEX, went up 0.80% to 3,444.55 points.

The narrower blue-chip CROBEX10 index climbed 0.40% to 2,180.40 points.

Zagrebacka Banka (ZABA) [ZSE:ZABA] was the most traded company with a turnover of some 291,000 euro ($300,000) and the biggest daily gainer. Its shares closed 5.26% higher at 28 euro after it said on Monday morning that that its consolidated net profit last year rose 9.2%, reaching 556 million euro. ZABA is part of the wider index.

Shares of hotel owner and manager Valamar [ZSE:RIVP] appreciated 1.27% to 6.36 euro in a turnover of 224,000 euro.

Shares of software developer Span [ZSE:SPAN] and Hrvatski Telekom [ZSE:HT] gained 1.18% and 0.47% respectively, as each of them generated a turnover of almost 120,000 euro.

Shares of index heavyweight electrical concern Koncar [ZSE:KOEI] went up 0.93% to 540 euro in a turnover of 83,000 euro. The company signed on Monday a deal to buy a 75% stake in local engineering company HELB.

The bourse's regular turnover fell to 1.2 million euro from 1.9 million euro on Friday.

($ = 0.969 euro)
