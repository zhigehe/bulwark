SARAJEVO (Bosnia and Herzegovina), February 12 (SeeNews) - Italian banking group Unicredit’s operations in Bosnia recorded a 26% increase in net profit to 112.5 million euro ($116.8 million) in 2024, the group said.

The group's two subsidiaries in Bosnia registered a 14% annual growth in revenue to 235 million euro last year, it said in an annual financial report published on Tuesday.

Customer loans went up 14.6% to 2.6 billion euro, while customer deposits reached 3.9 billion euro, up 8.7%, in 2024.

The banking group increased its revenue from net interest in Bosnia by 10.5% to 148 million euro last year. Income from fees went up 15.8% to 78 million euro in 2024.

Unicredit's two subsidiaries in Bosnia are Unicredit bank dd Mostar [SAJ:ZGBM], which operates in the Federation, and Unicredit bank ad Banja Luka [BANJ:NBLB], which operates in the Serb Republic.

The Federation and the Serb Republic are two autonomous entities that form Bosnia and Herzegovina.

($ = 0.963 euro)
