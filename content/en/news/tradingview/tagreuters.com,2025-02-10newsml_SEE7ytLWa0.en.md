LJUBLJANA (Slovenia), February 10 (SeeNews) - The main share index of the Ljubljana Stock Exchange (LJSE), the SBITOP, rose 1.45% to 1,952.32 points in the trading week ended on February 7, after it soared 3.59% in the previous trading week, the bourse said on Monday.

Index heavyweight, pharmaceuticals producer Krka [LJE:KRKG], dominated the trading last week with a turnover of 6.1 million euro ($6.3 million), as its share price increased 2.17% to 164.50 euro, the bourse said in a weekly report.

Hand tools manufacturer Unior [LJE:UKIG] was the biggest gainer last week. Its shares, which are not part of the main index, rose 7.25% to 7.40 euro.

Shares of chemicals producer Cinkarna Celje [LJE:CICG] appreciated 4.32% last week, to 29 euro. On Wednesday, the company said that its net profit surged to 22.8 million euro in 2024, up from 12.7 million euro in 2023.

Total trading turnover on the LJSE last week fell to 14.9 million euro in 1,198 transactions, down from 16.7 million euro in 1,234 transactions transactions in the previous trading week.

Last month LJSE had the highest trading volume for January in the last 15 years and the third highest monthly trading volume in the last three years, the bourse said. SBITOP gained 15% in January and is only 2% away from the 2,000-point mark, it added.

The benchmark equity index of the Ljubljana bourse rose by one-third in 2024.

($ = 0.970 euro)
