Timberland Bancorp Inc., operating primarily in the western Washington region under the name 'Timberland Bank', has released its latest Form 10-Q report, showcasing robust financial and operational performance. The report highlights significant growth in key financial metrics and provides insights into the company's business activities and future outlook.

**Financial Highlights**

**Total Interest and Dividend Income**: Timberland Bancorp Inc. reported a total interest and dividend income of $25.26 million, reflecting an increase from the previous year due to higher interest income from loans receivable.

**Net Interest Income**: The company's net interest income grew to $16.97 million, driven by increased interest income despite higher interest expenses.

**Net Income**: Timberland Bancorp Inc. achieved a net income of $6.86 million, an improvement over the prior year's $6.30 million, indicating stronger profitability.

**Basic Net Income Per Common Share**: The basic net income per common share rose to $0.86, up from $0.78 in the previous year, reflecting improved earnings per share.

**Diluted Net Income Per Common Share**: The diluted net income per common share increased to $0.86, compared to $0.77 in the previous year, indicating enhanced shareholder value.

**Business Highlights**

**Revenue Segments**: The company reported revenue from service charges on deposits, ATM and debit card interchange transaction fees, and escrow fees. These revenue streams are part of the company's non-interest income, which is recognized under ASC 606.

**Geographical Performance**: Timberland Bancorp Inc. operates primarily in the western Washington region under the name 'Timberland Bank'. The performance in this region is managed as a single strategic unit, indicating a focus on community banking services.

**Sales Units**: The company originated loans for sale amounting to $2,680,000 and had proceeds from sales of loans totaling $2,312,000 during the reporting period. This reflects the company's active participation in the loan origination and sales market.

**Future Outlook**: The company expects the fair value of its investment securities to recover as they approach maturity or if market yields decline. Additionally, Timberland Bancorp Inc. is monitoring economic conditions and their potential impact on credit quality and loan performance.
