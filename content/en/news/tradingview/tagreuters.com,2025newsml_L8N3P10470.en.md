Here are news, reports and events that may affect Turkish financial markets on Monday.

The lira was quoted at 35.9710-36.0690 against the U.S. dollar at 0441 GMT, after closing at 35.9790 on Friday.

The main BIST 100 share index closed 1.11% higher at 9,951.65 points on Friday.

GLOBAL MARKETS

Asian shares dithered and the dollar edged higher on Monday after U.S. President Donald Trump warned more tariffs were imminent including on steel and aluminium, an inflationary move that could limit the scope for rate cuts.

ERDOGAN IN MALAYSIA

President Tayyip Erdogan is making an official visit to Malaysia, where he will meet Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim. They are expected to discuss the reconstruction of Gaza and bilateral matters. Erdogan will subsequently visit Indonesia and Pakistan.

ERDOGAN ON TRUMP GAZA PLAN

Erdogan said on Sunday that there is no point in discussing what U.S. President Donald Trump has said about a plan to take over the war-ravaged Gaza Strip.

TRANSPORT MINISTER

Minister of Transport and Infrastructure Abdulkadir Uraloglu will attend a joint meeting of the United Nations Economic Commission for Europe in Geneva.

UNEMPLOYMENT DATA (TRUR=ECI)

The Turkish Statistical Institute will announce unemployment data for December. (0700 GMT)

INDUSTRIAL PRODUCTION DATA (TRIP=ECI)

The Turkish Statistical Institute will also release industrial production data for December. (0700 GMT)

MAIN OPPOSITION PARTY

Ozgur Ozel, leader of the main opposition Republican People's Party, will chair a meeting of the party assembly (0900 GMT)
