The Czech Republic’s current account posted a surplus of CZK 15.19 billion in December 2024, shifting from a CZK 7.29 billion deficit in the same month of the previous year and exceeding expectations of an CZK 8.9 billion excess.

The surplus in both the goods and services accounts widened to CZK 21.24 billion and CZK 9.42 billion, respectively, from CZK 18.14 billion and CZK 5.69 billion in December 2023.

Meanwhile, the primary income deficit narrowed significantly to CZK 9.74 billion from CZK 32.43 billion.

In contrast, the secondary income account recorded a deficit of CZK 5.72 billion, reversing from a CZK 1.30 billion surplus in the corresponding period last year.
