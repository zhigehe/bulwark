Germany’s wholesale prices rose by 0.9% year-on-year in January 2025, accelerating from a 0.1% gain in the previous month.

This marks the second consecutive period of growth and was the fastest since April 2023, mainly due to higher wholesale costs of non-ferrous ores, metals and semi-finished metal products (+24.4%).

Coffee, tea, cocoa, and spices also saw a significant price increase of 34.4%, while sugar, confectionery, and bakery products rose by 11%.

Milk, dairy products, eggs, edible fats, and oils were also up 7%.

In contrast, wholesale prices fell for iron, steel, and ferrous semi-finished metal products (-6.3%), as well as computers and peripheral equipment (-6.2%), and chemical products (-2.6%).

On a monthly basis, wholesales prices grew 0.9%, the most since September 2022, picking up from a 0.1% rise in December.
