Residential property prices in Indonesia climbed by 1.39% year-on-year in the fourth quarter of 2024, easing from a 1.46% rise in the previous quarter.

This was the softest growth in property prices since the fourth quarter of 2021, with prices moderating for both small houses (1.84% vs 1.97% in Q3) and medium houses (1.31% vs 1.33%).

By contrast, the growth price for large properties accelerated (1.46% vs 1.04%).

Among cities, property price moderation was most notable in Pontianak (2.82% vs 3.34% in Q3) and Banjarmasin (1.29% vs 1.57%), while prices accelerated in Surabaya (1.09% vs 0.73%), Balikpapan (1.22% vs 1.49%), and Pekanbaru (2.47% vs 2.64%)
