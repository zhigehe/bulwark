The PPI rose by 0.4% in January following a 0.5% gain in December, ahead of expectation, with energy prices up 1.7% and food prices up 1.1%.

Core prices, which exclude food and energy, rose by 0.3%, as expected, after a 0.4% increase in the previous month.

Services prices were up 0.3% in January, with trade services prices up 0.1%. Excluding food, energy and trade services prices, PPI rose by 0.3% after a 0.4% gain.

The year-over-year rise in producer prices remained at 3.5%, while core prices slowed to a 3.6% year-over-year rate after a 3.7% year-over-year gain in the previous month, still elevated. When trade services prices are also excluded, prices were up 3.4% after a 3.5% gain.

Initial jobless claims decreased by 7,000 to 213,000 in the week ended Feb. 8, reducing the four-week moving average by 1,000 to 216,000. Insured claims fell by 36,000 to 1.85 million in the week ended Feb. 1.

Natural gas stocks fell by 100 billion cubic feet to 2.297 trillion cubic feet in the week ended Feb. 7, down 9.7% from a year earlier and 2.8% lower than the seasonal average for the current week over the previous five years.
