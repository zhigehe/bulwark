Singapore's FTSE Straits Times Index rose 0.4% to close at 3875.13, after hitting a record intraday high of 3921.30 earlier in the session. Gains were supported by the city-state's three major lenders testing record-high share prices. OCBC and UOB closed shy of intraday highs, advancing 0.35% and 1.0%, respectively. DBS Group climbed 1.6% after posting a record annual profit and announcing additional shareholder returns for 2025. Among decliners, Hongkong Land fell 3.7%, Frasers Logistics & Commercial Trust slid 2.3% and Singapore Exchange was 2.1% lower. (kimberley.kao@wsj.com)
