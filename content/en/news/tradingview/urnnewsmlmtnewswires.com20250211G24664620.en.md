The bank holding company said earnings per share rose to 153.65 yen from 107.26 yen a year ago, according to a Tokyo Stock Exchange filing on Feb. 7.

Ordinary revenue increased 23% to 178.9 billion yen from 145.4 billion yen a year earlier.

Iyogin Holdings forecasted attributable profit to grow 26.7% to 50 billion yen or 167.72 yen per share and ordinary revenue to gain 13.1% to 218 billion yen for the fiscal year ending March 31.
