\*\* Shares of Unilever down 5.3% at 4,501p, among the top losers in FTSE 100 index CURRENCYCOM:UK100

\*\* Co to list its ice cream business in Amsterdam, London and New York

\*\* Some analysts preferred a clean sale or JV of the ice cream division, or a joint US/UK listing as it reduced the risk of flow-back - when investors sell shares in a foreign company back to investors who live in the country

\*\* Unilever expects a slower start to 2025 due to subdued market growth in the near term

\*\* Co's Q4 underlying sales growth misses ests at 4% vs company compiled estimate of a 4.1% growth

\*\* Co sees 2025 underlying sales growth to be within its multi-year range of 3% to 5% vs company-compiled estimate of a 4.3% growth

\*\* Unilever, the owner of the popular Magnum and Wall's brands, launches a share buyback programme worth 1.5 billion euros ($1.56 billion)

\*\* ULVR stock fell about 1% YTD

($1 = 0.9592 euros)
