\*\* Shares in Brazilian firms Cogna and Yduqs rise as Santander upgrades the former and chooses the latter as its "top pick" within the local education sector

\*\* Santander analysts upgrade Cogna to "outperform" from "neutral," citing "strong" earnings momentum, free cash flow generation and an "attractive valuation"

\*\* In a report dated Feb. 7, the analysts also cut Cogna's target price to 2 reais ($0.35) per share from 4 reais, suggesting an about 31% upside from Friday's closing

\*\* Analysts Caio Moscardini and Karoline Correia say Yduqs remains Santander's "top pick," citing factors such as its diversified portfolio and positive earnings momentum

\*\* Still, they also cut the target price for Yduqs, which Santander rates "outperform," to 14.70 reais from 22 reais, suggesting a 40% upside from Friday's closing price

\*\* Cogna shares are up 5.2% and Yduqs is up 4.9%; Cogna trades at its highest intra-day level since August while Yduqs reaches its highest since November

\*\* Both stocks are among the top gainers on Brazil's benchmark index Bovespa , which is up around 0.8%

($1 = 5.7799 reais)
