China's trade-in subsidies program for electronic products has attracted more than 20 million consumers since it was launched three weeks ago, Xinhua News Agency reported Sunday, citing the Commerce Ministry.

The consumers purchased more than 25.4 million units of electronics such as mobile phones, according to the report.

Beijing has provided up to 500 yuan of subsidies for the purchase of digital products, according to Xinhua.
