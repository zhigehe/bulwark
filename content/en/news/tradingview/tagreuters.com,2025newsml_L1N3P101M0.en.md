Key points:

* Korean won weakens against dollar
* South Korea benchmark bond yield rises

Round-up of South Korean financial markets:

\*\* South Korean shares inched marginally lower on Monday, dragged down by automakers and steelmakers, after U.S. President Donald Trump said he would introduce new tariffs on steel and aluminum imports.

\*\* The benchmark KOSPI was down 3.46 points, or 0.14%, at 2,518.46 as of 0131 GMT.

\*\* Trump on Sunday said he would introduce new 25% tariffs on all steel and aluminum imports, on top of existing metals duties, in another major escalation of his trade policy overhaul.

\*\* South Korea's acting President Choi Sang-mok discussed on Monday responses to Trump's proposed tariffs on steel and aluminum.

\*\* Steelmaker POSCO Holdings fell more than 3% to a more than two-year low, while Hyundai Steel dropped more than 2% to a record low.

\*\* Hyundai Motor shed 0.75%, while sister automaker Kia Corp lost 2.90%, amid worries that the auto sector might also be hit by Trump's trade policies.

\*\* Of the total 940 traded issues, 369 shares advanced, while 504 declined.

\*\* Foreigners were net sellers of shares worth 58.1 billion won ($39.97 million).

\*\* The won was quoted at 1,455.7 per dollar on the onshore settlement platform , 0.12% lower than its previous close at 1,454.0.

\*\* In money and debt markets, March futures on three-year treasury bonds (KTBc1) fell 0.06 point to 106.63.

\*\* The most liquid three-year Korean treasury bond yield (KR3YT=RR) rose 4.1 basis points to 2.672%, while the benchmark 10-year yield rose 3.8 bps to 2.854%.

($1 = 1,453.5700 won)
