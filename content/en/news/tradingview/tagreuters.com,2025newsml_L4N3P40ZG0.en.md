Deere & Co reported lower first-quarter profit on Thursday, as more farmers switched to renting tractors and other equipment due to weak incomes and high borrowing rates.

The world's largest farm-equipment maker reported a net income of $869 million, or $3.19 per share, compared with $1.75 billion, or $6.23 per share, a year earlier.
