Asian equities traded in the US as American depositary receipts kicked off the week sharply higher Monday morning, rising 1.11% to 2,190.94 on the S&P Asia 50 ADR Index.

From North Asia, the gainers were led by mobile big data platform Aurora Mobile and tech company Xunlei , which climbed 21% and 9.8% respectively. They were followed by fintech firm Jiayin Group and internet and data center provider VNET Group , which advanced 8 .1% and 6.5% respectively.

The decliners from North Asia were led by consumer lending firm Eason Technology and brand management company 36Kr , which fell 12% and 7.2% respectively. They were followed by diagnostic imaging centers company Concord Medical Services and consumer lending firm LexinFintech , which dropped 6% and 3.6% respectively.

From South Asia, the gainers were led by tech conglomerate Sea and financial services company ICICI Bank , which rose 1% and 0.7% respectively. They were followed by It firm Wipro and telecommunications operator PLDT , which were up 0.3% and 0.1% respectively.

The decliners from South Asia were led by telecommunications operator Telekomunikasi Indonesia , which lost 7% followed by IT firm Infosys and financial services company HDFC Bank , which were off 0.3% and 0.1% respectively.
