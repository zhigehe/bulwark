Here are news, reports and events that may affect Turkish financial markets on Tuesday.

The lira traded at 36.02 against the U.S. dollar at 0248 GMT, after closing at 35.9950 on Monday.

The main BIST 100 share index closed 1.06% lower at 9,845.85 points on Monday.

GLOBAL MARKETS

Gold hit a record high, the dollar was firm and Hong Kong shares advanced to a four-month peak on Tuesday as investors navigated shifts in U.S. trade policy and waited to hear from Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell on tariffs and inflation.

ERDOGAN IN MALAYSIA

President Tayyip Erdogan, who is making an official visit to Malaysia, will hold a joint press conference with Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim following their talks (0530 GMT). He will also attend the Turkey-Malaysia Business Forum.

TRANSPORT MINISTER

Transport Minister Abdulkadir Uraloglu will attend a transportation committee meeting of the United Nations Economic Commission for Europe in Geneva. He will also meet counterparts from the Netherlands and Iraq.

OPPOSITION

Main opposition CHP (1030 GMT) and pro-Kurdish DEM Party (0945 GMT) will hold their weekly parliamentary group meetings.

TREASURY AUCTIONS

The Treasury will reissue a five-month coupon-free bond due on July 23, 2025, and directly sell the fixed-rate lease certificate due on February 10, 2027.

UNEMPLOYMENT AND INDUSTRIAL PRODUCTION

Turkey's unemployment rate slipped 0.1 percentage points month-on-month to 8.5% in December, official data showed on Monday, while industrial production rose 5.0% in the same period on a seasonally and calendar-adjusted basis.
