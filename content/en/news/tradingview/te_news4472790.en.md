The annual inflation rate in Denmark eased 1.5% in January 2025, down from December’s seven-month high of 1.9%.

This was mainly due to a slowdown in prices for housing & utilities (1.9% vs 3.2% in December) and food & non-alcoholic beverages (4.4% vs 4.7%).

Prices also softened for health (1.4% vs 1.8%), communication (0.1% vs 2.3%), education (3.7% vs 3.9%), and restaurants & hotels (1.9% vs 2.6%).

Moreover, decreases were seen in costs for clothing & footwear (-0.8% vs 0.5%), furnishings, household equipment & routine household maintenance (-2.6% vs -3.5%), and recreation & culture (-0.2% vs -0.4%).

In contrast, prices rose for transport (1% vs 0.3%) and miscellaneous goods & services (2.2% vs 1.8%).

Meanwhile, the core inflation, which excludes energy and unprocessed food, edged down to 1.4% from 1.5%.

On a monthly basis, consumer prices increased by 0.6% in January, rebounding from a 0.3% fall in December.
