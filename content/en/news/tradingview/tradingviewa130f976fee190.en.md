Riverview Bancorp Inc., a regional bank operating primarily in Washington and Oregon, has released its Form 10-Q report for the third quarter. The report provides a detailed overview of the company's financial performance and operational highlights, reflecting both the challenges and opportunities faced by the bank in the current economic environment.

**Financial Highlights**

Riverview Bancorp Inc. reported the following key financial metrics for the third quarter:

* **Total Interest and Dividend Income**: $44.468 million, an increase from $42.264 million in the prior year period, driven by higher interest and fees on loans receivable.
* **Net Interest Income**: $27.151 million, a decrease from $29.534 million in the prior year period, primarily due to increased interest expense on deposits and borrowings.
* **Net Income**: $3.755 million, a decline from $6.767 million in the prior year period, impacted by higher non-interest expenses and a decrease in net interest income.
* **Basic EPS**: $0.18, compared to $0.32 in the prior year period, reflecting the decrease in net income.
* **Diluted EPS**: $0.18, consistent with Basic EPS, indicating no significant impact from potential dilutive securities.

**Business Highlights**

The company's business performance for the third quarter included the following highlights:

* **Revenue Segments**: The company reported interest and fees on loans receivable as a significant source of income, with notable increases compared to the previous year. This indicates a strong performance in the lending segment.
* **Geographical Performance**: The company's business activities are primarily concentrated in the states of Washington and Oregon, suggesting a regional focus that may influence operational strategies and market penetration.
* **Sales Units**: The loan portfolio showed a diverse range of products, including commercial business, commercial real estate, land, multi-family, real estate construction, and consumer loans. This diversity in loan types reflects the company's broad operational scope in the lending market.
* **Future Outlook**: Management's discussion highlighted a cautious approach to credit risk, with a focus on maintaining a strong allowance for credit losses. This indicates a forward-looking strategy to manage potential economic uncertainties and ensure financial stability.
