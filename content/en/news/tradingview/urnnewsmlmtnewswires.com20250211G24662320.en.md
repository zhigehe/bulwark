South Korean shares closed higher on Tuesday as investors appeared not to pay any heed to US President Donald Trump's new tariff plan announcements.

Foreigners and institutions bought 144.09 billion won and 245.73 billion won worth of domestic shares, respectively, while retail investors sold 472.61 billion won.

The Korea Composite Stock Price Index, or Kospi, was up by 17.78 points, or 0.7%, to close at 2,539.05. The Kosdaq decreased by 0.08 points, or 0.01%, to end at 749.59.

In economic news, South Korea's Financial Supervisory Service unveiled its strategic goals for 2025, focusing on stability, trust, growth, and innovation.

The financial watchdog plans to enhance financial system stability by supervising real estate finance and addressing potential risks while boosting loss-absorbing capacities. Efforts will also target combating unfair practices, boosting internal controls, and protecting consumers, according to an FSS release.

The FSS aims to foster a "virtuous" financial ecosystem, support capital market advancements, and safeguard vulnerable groups. It will also promote AI-driven digital innovation and establish a secure digital environment while continuing internal innovations to deliver advanced supervisory services.

In corporate news, LG Energy Solution was selected to manage the bidding operations for South Korea's largest offshore wind power facility, the Jeju Hanlim Offshore Wind Farm.

The 100-megawatt site, spanning 5.5 square kilometers off Jeju Island, houses 18 turbines and produces over 234,000 megawatt-hours of renewable energy on an annual basis. The battery manufacturer will oversee power generation forecasts and participate in Korea Power Exchange's bidding system to facilitate renewable energy distribution nationwide.

Shares of LG Energy Solution fell 1% at market close on Tuesday.
