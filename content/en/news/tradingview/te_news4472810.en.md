Household spending in Sweden fell by 0.3% month-over-month in December 2024, slipping from a 1.1% increase in the previous month.

Spending declined mainly for transport and retail sales and service of motor vehicles (-4.9% vs 0.9% in November), recreation and culture, goods and services (-0.4% vs 3.4%), and housing, electricity, gas and heating (-1.9% vs 1.7%).

Meanwhile, consumption increased for clothing and footwear (5.7% vs -3.4%) and food and beverages(0.9% vs 0.1%).

At the same time, spending decreased at a softer pace for furniture, furnishings, household equipment and consumables (-0.7% vs -0.8%).

On a yearly basis, household spending rose by 0.7%, easing from a 1.6% gain in the preceding period.
