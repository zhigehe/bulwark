Finland’s gross domestic product expanded by 0.9% year-on-year in December 2024, easing from a downwardly revised 1.2% rise in the previous month.

This marked the third consecutive month of growth albeit at a softer pace, as output slowed for both agriculture, forestry and fishing (17.8% vs 18.3% in November) and services (0.02% vs 0.7%).

Meanwhile, output increased for secondary production (1.9% vs 0.9%).

On a seasonally adjusted monthly basis, the GDP stagnated in December, following a downwardly revised 0.2% gain in the preceding period.
