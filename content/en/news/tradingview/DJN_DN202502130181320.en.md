Chinese shares are fluctuating between mild gains and losses after opening mostly lower. Investors are digesting President Trump's next move on tariffs after a delay of his long-threatened reciprocal tariffs. Pharmaceutical and auto stocks are leading the gains. Jiangsu Hengrui Medicine is up 1.4% and WuXi AppTec is 3.8% higher. BYD increases 1.4% and Great Wall Motor is 2.05% higher. Property stocks are broadly lower with Poly Developments & Holdings Group down 1.2% and China Vanke falling 2.0%. The benchmark Shanghai Composite Index is down 0.2% at 3326.94, the Shenzhen Composite Index is 0.2% lower and the ChiNext Price Index is up 0.4%. (sherry.qin@wsj.com)
