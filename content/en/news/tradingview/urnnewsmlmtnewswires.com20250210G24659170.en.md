South Korea and Australia signed a memorandum of understanding to establish a zero-emission shipping route by 2029, using environment-friendly technologies and non-carbon fuels, Yonhap News reported Monday, citing the Ministry of Oceans and Fisheries.

Oceans Minister Kang Do-hyung highlighted the initiative as key to achieving carbon neutrality in the shipping industry, the report said.

The two countries will also collaborate on developing green digital technologies and supporting the global shipping sector's goal of carbon neutrality by 2050, it said.
