Retail sales in Indonesia increased by 1.8% year-on-year in December 2024, accelerating from November’s 11-month low of 0.9% and marking the fastest pace since last September.

The latest reading also marked the eighth consecutive month of expansion in retail trade.

Sales of automotive parts and accessories rose sharply (13.0% vs 7.2% in November), while those of food (2.3% vs 2.5%), fuels (3.9% vs 8.8%), and clothing (0.6% vs 0.6%) all continued to increase.

Additionally, sales of information and communication (-5.9% vs -23.1%), cultural and recreational goods (-0.9% vs -2.4%), and home appliances (-6.8% vs -7.2%) all fell at a softer pace.

Retail sales are expected to climb by 0.4% in January.

On a monthly basis, retail sales grew by 5.9% in December, the most in nine months, rebounding from a 0.4% drop in November.
