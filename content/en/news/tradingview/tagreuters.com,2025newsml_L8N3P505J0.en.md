Here are news, reports and events that may affect Turkish financial markets on Friday.

The lira traded at 36.1960 against the U.S. dollar at 0321 GMT, after closing at 36.1600 on Thursday.

The main BIST 100 share index closed 1.04% lower at 9,914.68 points on Thursday.

GLOBAL MARKETS

Asian stocks tracked Wall Street higher on Friday, while the dollar eased as investors contended with U.S. President Donald Trump's reciprocal tariffs that were not immediately imposed as well as U.S. data allaying investor worries on inflation.

ERDOGAN ON TRUMP GAZA PLAN

President Tayyip Erdogan said on Thursday that U.S. President Trump's plan to displace Palestinians from Gaza posed a major threat to world peace.

ERDOGAN RETURNS FROM ASIA TRIP

President Erdogan arrived back in Istanbul on Friday after a trip to Malaysia, Indonesia and Pakistan.

CENTRAL BANK SURVEY (TRINTM=ECI)

The central bank will release its monthly survey of market participants' expectations for the economy. (0700 GMT)

HOUSE SALES

The Turkish Statistical Institute will announce house sales data for January. (0700 GMT)

MUNICH SECURITY CONFERENCE

Foreign Minister Hakan Fidan will attend the Munich Security Conference.
