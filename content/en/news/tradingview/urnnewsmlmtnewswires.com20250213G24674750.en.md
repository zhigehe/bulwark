Malaysian shares ended in the red zone, reversing gains of the previous session and mirroring Wall Street's overnight losses.

The benchmark FTSE Bursa Malaysia KLCI, the main gauge of Malaysian stocks, shed 10.77 points to end 0.67% lower at 1,592.28. The day range was between 1,592.20 and 1,605.48.

In corporate news, shares of Heineken Malaysia gained about 5% after its profit attributable to owners rose 42% to 140.9 million ringgit in the fourth quarter of 2024 from 99.1 million ringgit a year earlier.

Shares of YBS International slid over 1% after it completed the second and final tranche of the private placement after listing and quotation of 13.7 million placement shares on the ACE Market of Bursa Securities.

Shares of Malaysian Pacific Industries also slid over 1% after its unit Dynacraft Industries proposed to sell two parcels of land with the buildings in Pinang, Malaysia, to Open DC PE2 Asset for 140 million ringgit.
