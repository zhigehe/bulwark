The Canadian dollar strengthened against the greenback on Thursday, and the yield on benchmark government debt slipped.

The loonie was trading 0.1% higher at C$1.4285 to the greenback, or 70 U.S. cents, after trading in a range of 1.4256 to 1.4306.

Canadian government 10-year bond yields (CA10YT=RR) fell 5 basis points to 3.129%. The yield on similar U.S. government benchmark debt fell to 4.566%.

U.S. March crude futures fell 97 cents to $70.4 a barrel on Thursday.
