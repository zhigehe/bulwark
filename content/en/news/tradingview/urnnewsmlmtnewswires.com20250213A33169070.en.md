Asian equities traded in the US as American depositary receipts were higher Thursday morning, rising 0.4% to 2,203.14 on the S&P Asia 50 ADR Index.

From North Asia, the gainers were led by semiconductor company Himax Technologies and consumer lending firm Eason Technology , which climbed 6.9% and 5.4% respectively. They were followed by semiconductor company ASE Technology and biotech firm Zai Lab , which advanced 5.4% and 4.7% respectively.

The decliners from North Asia were led by media company 36Kr and mobile big data platform Aurora Mobile , which fell 4.4% and 3.4% respectively. They were followed by automotive ecommerce platform Cango and computer hardware maker Canaan , which dropped 2.7% and 2.2% respectively.

From South Asia, the gainers were led by pharmaceutical company Dr. Reddy's Laboratories , which increased 1.2%, followed by financial services company ICICI Bank and telecommunications operator Telekomunikasi Indonesia , which were up 0.3% and 0.2% respectively.

The decliners from South Asia were led by IT firm Infosys and telecommunications operator PLDT , which were down 0.6% and 0.5% respectively. They were followed by Wipro and financial services company HDFC Bank , which were off 0.4% and 0.2% respectively.
