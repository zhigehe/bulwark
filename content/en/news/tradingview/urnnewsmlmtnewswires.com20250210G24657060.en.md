China's National Financial Regulatory Administration rolled out a pilot program allowing 10 insurers to invest in gold as part of their asset allocation programs, Xinhua News Agency reported Friday.

The insurers will be allowed to invest in gold using various means, including spot contracts for gold traded on the Shanghai Gold Exchange main board, deferred gold delivery contracts centralized pricing scheme, spot inquiry contracts, swap contracts for gold inquiries, and gold leasing operations, the report said.
