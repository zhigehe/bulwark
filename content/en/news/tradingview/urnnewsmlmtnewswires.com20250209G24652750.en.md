Daiseki Eco. Solution will launch a plastic recycling business in Shizuoka City, Japan, aiming to convert household and commercial plastic waste into recycled materials, according to a Friday filing on the Tokyo Stock Exchange.

The waste treatment firm secured a city-backed contract to process 20,000 tonnes of plastic annually.

Daiseki Eco. Solution acquired a 30,331-square-meter site in Mariko, Suruga-ku, for 1.6 billion yen and will construct processing and recycling facilities. Operations are set to begin between fiscal 2028 and 2030.

Daiseki Eco. will collaborate with Shizuoka City under an agreement promoting plastic resource circulation.
