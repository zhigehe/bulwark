Chinese shares are mixed after opening higher. The benchmark Shanghai Composite Index is flat at 3303,77, the Shenzhen Composite Index is flat, while the ChiNext Price Index drops 0.6%. Steel stocks are down marginally after President Trump said he plans to impose 25% tariff on all aluminum and steel imports. Baoshan Iron & Steel is off 0.7% and Inner Mongolia Baotou Steel Union drops 0.5%. Meanwhile, consumer and telecom stocks advance. Yonghui Superstores rises 2.2% and CCOOP Group is 3.4% higher. Telcos rise broadly after integrating DeepSeek into their offerings. China Mobile is up 1.4% and China Telecom advances 4.35%.(sherry.qin@wsj.com)
