* Online fast-fashion giant Shein could delay a potential initial public offering in London after President Trump announced a crackdown on tariff-free imports of small goods from China, The Financial Times reports, citing unnamed sources
* The potential listing, which Shein had planned to take place as soon as this Easter, could be pushed into the second half of the year, according to the FT.
* The uncertainty over impact and timing of regulatory changes, which has temporarily paused small-good imports, is weighing on Shein's IPO timetable, the FT reports.
