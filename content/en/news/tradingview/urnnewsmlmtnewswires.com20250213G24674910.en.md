Shriram Finance has secured a 393 million euros and $100 million external commercial borrowing facility backed by Italy's export credit agency SACE, according to a Thursday filing with the Indian bourses.

The 10-year loan will finance Italian vehicle purchases under the company's Social Finance Framework.

The deal saw participation from HSBC, Deutsche Bank, KfW IPEX-Bank, ING Bank, and J.P. Morgan as lead arrangers, according to the filing.

This brings Shriram Finance's offshore funding for the current fiscal year to over $2.5 billion, the company said in the filing.
