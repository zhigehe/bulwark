Malaysia logged a current account surplus of 11.4 billion ringgit in the fourth quarter of 2024, according to data from the Department of Statistics Malaysia released Friday.

The surplus is attributable to net exports of goods and a lower services deficit, the department said.

In the third quarter, the current account surplus was lower at 2.2 billion ringgit and 900 million ringgit in the fourth quarter of 2023.

Malaysia's financial account outflow fell to 5.8 billion ringgit in the fourth quarter from 7.5 billion ringgit in the previous quarter.

Foreign direct investment inflows reached 18.4 billion ringgit, up from 14.5 billion ringgit in the previous quarter.

For 2024, the country's current account balance recorded a surplus of 32.8 billion ringgit.
