More than 30,000 basic-level smart factories were constructed in China, Xinhua News reported Wednesday, citing data from the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology.

China, which aims to boost industrial digitalization, also constructed 1,200 advanced-level and 230 excellence-level smart factories, with the latter covering 80% of the manufacturing industry, the report said.

The MIIT's data showed that these factories are 50.2% lower in defect rates on average and 28.4% shorter in product development cycles. They are also 22.3% higher when it comes to production efficiency and 20.4% lower in carbon emissions, the report said.
