India's Narayana Hrudayalaya has accepted bids worth 5 billion rupees ($57.5 million) for bonds maturing in five years, three bankers said on Friday.

It will pay an annual coupon of 8.40% on this issue and had invited bids from bankers and investors earlier in the day, they said.

The issue has a put/call option at end of three years and six months.

The company did not immediately reply to a Reuters email seeking comment.
