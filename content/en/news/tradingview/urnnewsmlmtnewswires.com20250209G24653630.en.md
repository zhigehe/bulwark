The Chiba Bank's profit attributable to owners of the parent rose 8.7% to 54.5 billion yen in the fiscal nine months ended Dec. 31,Â 2024, from 50.1 billion yen a year ago.

The Japanese regional bank's earnings per share rose to 76.31 yen from 69.27 yen a year ago, according to a filing.

Ordinary income grew 13.2% to 262.6 billion yen from 231.8 billion yen a year earlier.

The lender forecasted attributable profit to grow 12.1% to 70 billion yen or 98.20 yen per share and net sales to gain 9.4% to 98.8 billion yen for the fiscal year ending March 2025.

In a separate filing, Chiba Bank raised its dividend forecast to align with its payout policy.

It now plans to pay a year-end dividend of 22 yen per share (from 18 yen per share earlier), bringing the total annual dividend to 40 yen per share.
