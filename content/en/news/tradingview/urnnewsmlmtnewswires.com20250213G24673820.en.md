South Korean stocks hit a three-month high on Thursday amid hopes of US tariff exemption.

The Korea Composite Stock Price Index, or Kospi, was up by 34.78 points, or 1.4%, to close at 2,583.17, the best finish since Nov. 4,Â 2024. The Kosdaq rose 4.1 points, or 0.6%, to 749.28.

In economic news, South Korea's money supply increased in December 2024, with the broad money, or M2, rising 1% to 4,183.5 trillion won from 4,143 trillion won a month earlier.

M2 is a gauge of the money supply that encompasses cash, demand deposits, and other readily convertible financial instruments.

The country's narrow money, or M1, increased 2% to 1,274.3 trillion won in the month from 1,248.9 trillion won in November.

In corporate news, shares of Hanwha jumped nearly 12% after the company posted a net loss attributable to shareholders of 4.5 billion won in the fourth quarter of 2024, narrowing from 118.4 billion won a year earlier.
