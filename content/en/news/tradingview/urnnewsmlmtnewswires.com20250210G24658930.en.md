Australian consumer confidence eased last week, with the measure for current financial situation at its weakest since late-2024, according to a survey by ANZ Research and Roy Morgan Research.

The ANZ-Roy Morgan Australian Consumer Confidence Index fell to 86.7 points from 88.5 points in the week of Feb. 3 to 9, bringing the four-week moving average to 86.8.

The "current financial conditions" subindex declined to 71.2 from 73.9, its lowest level since late September 2024, per the report. The year-ahead financial situation measure rose to 105.9 from 105.1.

The measure for economic conditions over the next 12 months fell to 82.8 from 84.8, while the five-year counterpart dropped to 92 from 95.8.

The "time to buy a major household item" subindex was down to 81.8 from 82.7.

Lastly, the "weekly inflation expectations" measure rose to 4.7% from 4.6%, while the four-week moving average fell 0.1 percentage points to 4.9%.
