Nisshin Seifun Group's profit attributable to owners of the parent rose 3.5% to 30.5 billion yen in the first nine months of fiscal 2025, from 29.5 billion yen a year ago.

The food manufacturer's earnings per share increased to 102.69 yen from 99.26 yen a year ago, according to a Tokyo Stock Exchange filing on Feb. 7.

Net sales declined 0.5% to 647.4 billion yen from 650.7 billion yen a year earlier.

Nisshin Seifun forecasted attributable profit to grow 22.9% to 39 billion yen or 131.16 yen per share and net sales to gain 1.4% to 870 billion yen for the fiscal year ending March 31.

It expects to pay a year-end dividend of 30 yen, bringing the total annual dividend to 55 yen per share.
