South Korea will invest 208.8 billion won in 2025 to strengthen its global "technological competitiveness," marking a 25.9% increase from last year, Yonhap News reported last week.

The budget includes 94.2 billion won for bilateral and multilateral research and development initiatives, such as Eureka and Eurostars, and 105.2 billion won for joint research centers and future growth tech projects, the report said.

An additional 3.5 billion won will promote foreign investment and local-global R&D collaborations, it said.
