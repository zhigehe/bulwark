\*\* London-listed shares of Unilever fall 7% to 4,403p; among top losers on FTSE 100 index CURRENCYCOM:UK100

\*\* Stock set for worst day in over three years, if losses hold

\*\* ULVR to demerge its ice cream business and picks Amsterdam as its primary listing venue; London and New York as secondary listing

\*\* Some analysts preferred a clean sale or JV of the ice cream division, or a joint US/UK listing as it reduced the risk of flow-back - when investors sell shares in a foreign company back to investors who live in the country

\*\* Unilever expects slower start to 2025 due to subdued market growth in near term

\*\* Co's Q4 underlying sales growth misses ests at 4% vs company-compiled estimate of 4.1% growth

\*\* Co sees 2025 underlying sales growth to be within its multi-year range of 3%-5% vs company-compiled estimate of 4.3% growth

\*\* Unilever launches share buyback programme worth 1.5 billion euros ($1.56 billion)

\*\* ULVR stock fell about ~3.5% YTD

($1 = 0.9592 euros)
