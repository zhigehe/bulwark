British American Tobacco (, BTI.JO) said Thursday that it swung to a profit for full-year 2024, while revenue fell year over year.

Profit attributable to shareholders for the 12 months ended Dec. 31,Â 2024, was 3.07 billion pounds sterling, compared with an attributable loss of 14.19 billion pounds earlier. EPS was 1.36 pounds from a per-share loss of 6.466 pounds.

The tobacco products company's revenue was 25.87 billion pounds, compared with 27.28 billion pounds a year ago.

Analysts polled by FactSet were expecting 26.03 billion pounds in sales, 8.03 billion pounds in net income, and 3.64 pounds in EPS.

Meanwhile, the board declared an interim dividend of 2.4024 pounds per share for 2024, payable in equal installments in May 2025, August 2025, November 2025, and February 2026. It paid 2.3552 pounds per share.

The upcoming first installment of the dividend will be paid on May 7 to shareholders on record on March 28.

For 2025, British American Tobacco expects revenue growth of 1% and said it remains committed to dividend growth.
