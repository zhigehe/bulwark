The annual inflation rate in Belarus was at 5.2% in January of 2025, remaining unchanged from the 14-month low in the previous month to halt four consecutive periods of slowdowns.

Inflation was underpinned by grocery prices (6.5%), which offset inflation for non-food goods (3.3%).

In the meantime, price growth for services (5.2%) was in line with that of goods.

From the previous month, Belarussian consumer prices rose by 0.7%.
