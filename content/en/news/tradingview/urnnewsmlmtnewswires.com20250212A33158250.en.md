Financial stocks fell in Wednesday afternoon trading with the NYSE Financial Index and the Financial Select Sector SPDR Fund (XLF) both down 0.5%.

The Philadelphia Housing Index dropped 2.1%, and the Real Estate Select Sector SPDR Fund (XLRE) shed 0.6%.

Bitcoin (BTC/USD) increased 1.2% to $96,850, and the yield for 10-year US Treasuries jumped 10.2 basis points to 4.64%.

In economic news, the US seasonally adjusted consumer price index rose by 0.5% in January, above expectations for a 0.3% increase in a survey compiled by Bloomberg and following a 0.4% increase in December, according to data released by the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

In sector news, the White House is considering downsizing and consolidating US bank regulators, The Wall Street Journal reported Tuesday.

In corporate news, Zillow shares fell 7.3%. The company forecast Q1 revenue that trailed estimates by analysts.

Upstart shares jumped 31% after the company swung to an adjusted profit in Q4 as sales increased, and 2025 revenue guidance beat expectations.

Mercury General shares rose 8.9% after Raymond James upgraded its rating on the stock to strong buy from outperform.
