\*\* Qantas shares slip 1.5% to A$9.365, after falling as much as 2.9% earlier in the day, marking their worst intraday drop since February 3

\*\* Macquarie downgrades stock rating to "neutral" from "out-perform"

\*\* Brokerage says FY26 outlook shows signs of competition intensifying with Qatar entering the local market amid a lower currency environment and lower oil crack

\*\* Operationally international also starts to face some more yield pressure from Europe and U.S. routes as capacity grows - brokerage

\*\* Macquarie sees QAN resuming dividends due to a favourable oil price environment, forecasting a dividend of A$0.15 apiece

\*\* Lifts PT to A$9.3 from A$8.4

\*\* Stock up 5% last month, in line with AXJO's gains of 4.6%
