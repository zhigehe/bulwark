STOCKS:

The benchmark BSE Sensex was down 0.7% at 77,311.80, while the broader NSE Nifty 50 index declined 0.76% to 23,381.60, as U.S. President Donald Trump's tariff threats unnerved investors.

RUPEE:

The Indian rupee dropped 0.06% versus the U.S. dollar to 87.48, hurt by mounting tariff worries and persistent investor outflows.

GOVERNMENT BONDS:

The benchmark 10-year bond (IN067934G=CC) was quoted at 100.53 rupees, with the yield up about 1 basis point at 6.7128%, as investors continued to agonize over the Reserve Bank of India's monetary policy decision.

OVERNIGHT INDEX SWAPS:

The one-year overnight index swap rate (INR1YMIBROIS=CC) edged up 1 bp to 6.3575%, while the five-year swap rate (INR5YMIBROIS=CC) rose over 3 bps to 6.15%.

CALL MONEY:

India's overnight call money rate (INROND=) was at 5.80% and the overnight TREPS rate (INTREPTOT=TCCL) was at 6.18%.
