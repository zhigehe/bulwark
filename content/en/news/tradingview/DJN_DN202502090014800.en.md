New Zealand's NZX-50 closed 0.2% lower at 12876.35, dragged into the red by shares of its largest companies. The benchmark index spent all of Monday's session in negative territory, following a negative lead by U.S. equities at the end of last week. Shares of six of New Zealand's seven largest companies by market capitalization finished lower. Power companies Meridian Energy and Contact Energy lost 0.2% and 0.5%, respectively. Medical-device manufacturer Fisher & Paykel Healthcare dropped 0.4%, airport operator Auckland International lost 0.3%, infrastructure investor Infratil gave up 1.0% and logistics operator Mainfreight shed 0.9%. (stuart.condie@wsj.com)
