Storebrand launched the 750 million-Norwegian-krone first tranche of its share repurchase program, according to a Wednesday release.

The Norwegian insurer will buy back up to a total of 35,011,774 shares under the program, which is slated to end on June 27. The company on Monday secured regulatory approval to initiate share buybacks of up to 1.5 billion kroner in 2025.

The second tranche of the buyback program is expected to commence in the second half, subject to Storebrand's solvency margin surpassing 175%.
