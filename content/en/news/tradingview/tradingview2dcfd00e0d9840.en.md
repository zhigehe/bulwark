Construction Partners, Inc., a leading provider of construction services for public and private infrastructure projects, has released its latest Form 10-Q report, detailing significant financial and operational performance for the three months ended December 31, 2024. The report highlights robust revenue growth driven by increased demand in both public and private sectors, alongside key operational developments and future outlooks.

**Financial Highlights**

* **Revenues:** $561.6 million, reflecting a significant increase from the previous year's $396.5 million, driven by higher demand in both public and private infrastructure projects.
* **Gross Profit:** $76.6 million, up from $51.9 million in the prior year, indicating improved cost management and operational efficiency.
* **Operating Income:** $13.8 million, a decrease from $16.7 million last year, primarily due to increased acquisition-related expenses.
* **Net Income (Loss):** $(3.1) million, compared to a net income of $9.8 million in the previous year, impacted by higher interest expenses and acquisition costs.
* **Net Income (Loss) Per Share - Basic:** $(0.06), down from $0.19, reflecting the net loss incurred during the period.

**Business Highlights**

* **Revenue Segments:** The company derives a significant portion of its revenues from contracts with customers, predominantly by performing construction services for both public and private infrastructure projects. Public infrastructure projects accounted for 57.7% of consolidated revenues, while private projects contributed 42.3% for the three months ended December 31, 2024.
* **Geographical Performance:** Projects performed for various departments of transportation accounted for 33.5% of consolidated revenues for the three months ended December 31, 2024. The Florida Department of Transportation was a significant customer in the previous year, contributing 12.6% of revenues.
* **Sales Units:** The company reported significant increases in contracts receivable including retainage, net, which rose to $384,076,000 as of December 31, 2024, indicating a strong pipeline of ongoing projects.
* **New Production Launches:** The acquisition of Lone Star Paving added 10 HMA plants, four aggregate facilities, and one liquid asphalt terminal, significantly expanding the company's production capacity in Texas.
* **Future Outlook:** The company expects to earn approximately $1.4 billion in revenue from its unsatisfied or partially unsatisfied performance obligations under construction project contracts during the remainder of the fiscal year ending September 30, 2025, with an additional $0.7 billion thereafter.
