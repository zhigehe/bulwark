SOFIA (Bulgaria), February 10 (SeeNews) - Bulgaria's industrial production index fell by an annual 3.9% in December after adding a revised 2.4% in November, the statistical office said on Monday, citing preliminary and seasonally-adjusted data.

The biggest annual decline was posted in the output of electricity, gas, steam and air conditioning supply, at 28.8%, while the mining and quarrying industry registered a production increase of 8% on the year, the National Statistical Office (NSI) said in a monthly report.

On a monthly comparison basis, Bulgaria's industrial output went down 1.6% in the last month of 2024, following a revised slip of 0.4% in November.

Electricity, gas, steam and air conditioning supply recorded a monthly production drop of 7.4%, while mining and quarrying industry output jumped 21.4%.

Below are figures on changes in industrial output in December and November, breakdown by sector (in pct):

|  | Dec y/y | Dec m/m | Nov y/y\* | Nov m/m\* |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Total | -3.9 | -1.6 | 2.4 | -0.4 |
| -mining, quarrying | 8.0 | 21.4 | -5.0 | 1.5 |
| -manufacturing | -1.2 | -1.6 | 3.2 | -0.1 |
| -utilities | -28.8 | -7.4 | 11.7 | -8.0 |

\*revised
