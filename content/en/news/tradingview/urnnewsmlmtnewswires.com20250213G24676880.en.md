Kubota's profit attributable to owners of the parent fell 3.4% to 230.4 billion yen in the fiscal year ended Dec. 31, 2024, from 238.5 billion yen a year ago​ amid decreased revenue.

The agricultural machinery company's earnings per share declined to 197.61 yen from 201.74 yen a year ago, according to a Tokyo bourse filing​ on Thursday.

Revenue declined 0.1% to 3.016 trillion yen from 3.021 trillion yen a year earlier​.

Kubota forecasted attributable profit to fall 15% to 196.00 billion yen, or 170.52 yen per share, and revenue to rise 1.1% to 3.05 trillion yen for the fiscal year ending Dec. 31​.

The company declared a year-end dividend of 25 yen per share, bringing the total annual dividend to 50 yen per share​.

The company expects to maintain interim and year-end dividends of 25 yen per share each for the current fiscal year​.

Kubota shares recently shed over 3% recently.
