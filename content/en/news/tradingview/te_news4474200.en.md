Retails sales in Turkey eased to 13.5% in December 2024 from an upwardly revised and eight-month high of 16.6% gain in the previous month.

This marked the weakest expansion in retail trade since July, as sales slowed for information and communications equipment in specialized stores (15.4% vs 17.3% in November), other household equipment in specialized stores (6.8% vs 10.5%), sales of other goods in specialized stores (26.7% vs 34.4%), sales via stalls/markets (32% vs 35.1%), and trade not in stores, stalls or markets (9.5% vs 15%).

In addition, sales continued to decrease for automotive fuel in specialized stores (-3% vs 0.4%).

Meanwhile, sales accelerated for food, beverages and tobacco in specialized stores (16.9% vs 16.5%), cultural and recreation goods in specialized stores (9% vs 7%), and sales in non-specialized stores (10.6% vs 10.2%).

On a seasonally adjusted monthly basis, retail trade slowed to 0.4% in December from 1.9% in the previous month.
