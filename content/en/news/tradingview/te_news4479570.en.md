Lithuania’s annual inflation rate accelerated to 3.6% in January 2025, up from 2.1% in the previous month.

This marked the highest reading since September 2023, mainly due to a broad increase in prices for food & non-alcoholic beverages (2.6% vs 1.4% in December), and transport (5.5% vs 1.1%).

Costs also rose faster for alcoholic beverages and tobacco (7.0% vs 5.5%), recreation and culture (4.8% vs 3.7%), and miscellaneous goods & services (4.4% vs 4.0%).

Additionally, prices declined less for housing & utilities (-0.1% vs -3.6%) and clothing and footwear (-1.8% vs -2.0%).

Meanwhile, costs strongly rebounded for furnishing & household equipment (1.1% vs -1.0%), and inflation slowed for communication (1.9% vs 2.2%) and restaurants & hotels (7.1% vs 8.9%).

On a monthly basis, consumer prices jumped by 1.8%, reaching over a two-year high, and accelerating from a 0.2% gain in the preceding period.
