Asian equities traded in the US as American depositary receipts were trending lower Wednesday morning, declining 0.36% to 2,185.64 on the S&P Asia 50 ADR Index.

From North Asia, the gainers were led by online brokerage UP Fintech and consumer lending firm Eason Technology and , which climbed 7.9% and 7%, respectively. They were followed by fintech firm Jiayin Group and streaming video service iQIYI , which advanced 6.3% and 6.1%, respectively.

The decliners from North Asia were led by financial services company Nomura and e-commerce company JD.com , which fell 5.2% and 3.9%, respectively. They were followed by online discount retailer Vipshop and semiconductor company Himax Technologies , which dropped 3.2% and 2.8%, respectively.

From South Asia, the only gainers were telecommunications operators Telekomunikasi Indonesia and PLDT , which rose 2.3% and 0.8%, respectively.

The decliners from South Asia were led by IT firms Sify Technologies and Infosys , which lost 3.4% and 2%, respectively. They were followed by IT firm Wipro and tech conglomerate Sea , which were down 1.8% and 0.7%, respectively.
