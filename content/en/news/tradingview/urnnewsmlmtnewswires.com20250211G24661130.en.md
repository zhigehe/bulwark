Zhejiang Southeast Space Frame plans to invest 500 million yuan in the construction of an agricultural photovoltaic project, according to a Shenzhen Stock Exchange disclosure on Tuesday.

The Chinese large-scale building integration service provider's subsidiary, Zhejiang Xingneng Technology, will manage the construction of the Xiaoshan (Puyang) Zero Carbon Taoyuanli Photovoltaic Power Station.

The project will be located in Hangzhou City, Zhejiang Province, China, and will have a capacity of 130 megawatt peaks on the DC side and 110 megawatts on the AC side.

The construction will take 14 months to complete, with the grid-connected power generation expected to be completed in 2026, the disclosure said.

The company's shares rose more than 1% in recent trade.
