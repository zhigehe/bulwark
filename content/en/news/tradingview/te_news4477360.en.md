Turkey’s current account deficit widened sharply to $4.65 billion in December 2024 from $1.71 billion in the corresponding month of the previous year, above market expectations of $4 billion shortfall.

This marked the largest trade gap since April 2024, as goods account deficit increased to $6.24 billion from $4.54 billion in December 2023, while primary income shortfall went up significantly to $1.67 billion from $0.24 billion.

In addition, secondary income surplus fell to $0.11 billion in December 2024 from $0.15 billion a year earlier.

Meanwhile, services account surplus rose to $3.14 billion from $2.92 billion.
