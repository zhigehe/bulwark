BUCHAREST (Romania), February 10 (SeeNews) - Romania's finance ministry sold on Monday 1.029 billion lei ($213.6 million/206.7 million euro) worth of fixed-rate government bonds maturing on July 29, 2030, above target, as the issue was more than twice oversubscribed, central bank data showed.

The average accepted yield fell to 7.39% at Monday's auction, from 7.75% achieved in the previous auction of Treasury paper from the same issue held last month. Demand for the government bonds with an annual coupon of 6.85% rose to 1.382 billion lei from 716.2 million lei at the previous auction.

The issue will be reopened on Wednesday when the finance ministry hopes to raise an additional 90 million lei through a non-competitive tender.

Details follow:

| Auction date | Feb 10, 2025 | Jan 16, 2025 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Amount offered (mln lei) | 600.0 | 400.0 |
| Amount sold (mln lei) | 1,028.8 | 400.0 |
| Total bids placed (mln lei) | 1,381.8 | 716.2 |
| Bid-to-cover ratio | 1.34 | 1.79 |
| Yield (%) | 7.39 | 7.75 |

Romania's finance ministry has sold about 11.764 billion lei worth of government securities year to date.

(1 euro = 4.9765 lei)
