Australia's new investment loans for dwellings declined 0.4% to 132,082 in the December 2024 quarter, marking the dataset's first fall since the March 2023 quarter, the Australian Bureau of Statistics reported Wednesday.

The number of new owner occupier loan commitments rose 2.2% to 83,206. Loan commitments for first home buyers went up 1.3%, while those for non-first home buyers rose 1.2%. Investor loan commitments totaled 48,876, down 4.5%.

In value terms, total new loan commitments for dwellings rose 1.4%.

Compared with the year-earlier period, the number of new investment loans for dwellings grew 7.2% in the December 2024 quarter.
