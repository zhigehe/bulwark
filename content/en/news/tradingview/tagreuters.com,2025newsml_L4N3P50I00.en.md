The following are the top stories on the New York Times business pages. Reuters has not verified these stories and does not vouch for their accuracy.

- TikTok returned on the U.S. app stores of Apple and Google on Thursday, as President Donald Trump delayed its ban until April 5 and assured the companies they would not be fined for distributing or maintaining the Chinese app.

- Banks including Morgan Stanley on Thursday sold about $4.7 billion of X’s debt according to two people familiar with the transaction.

- Co-founder of Airbnb Joe Gebbia is taking a role in Elon Musk's Department of Government Efficiency, according to a person with knowledge of the matter.

- Transportation Secretary Sean Duffy on Thursday said that he had asked Kelly Ortberg, CEO of Boeing to come to Washington and update him on the company’s commitment to addressing its quality and safety issues.
