Livestock futures on the CME closed trading mixed, with most-active live cattle finishing up 0.7% to $1.987 a pound, and lean hogs closing down 0.6% to 91.85 cents a pound. For hogs, early strength was quickly shrugged off amid higher volatility in the world trade stage, but the slaughter shortfall seen in U.S. pork looks to provide support for pork cutouts and CME futures in sessions ahead. "The shortfall in slaughter has impacted spot availability, with some items, such as bellies, being particularly sensitive," says Steiner Consulting Group in a note. Through midday, the USDA reported pork cutouts higher across the board. (kirk.maltais@wsj.com)
