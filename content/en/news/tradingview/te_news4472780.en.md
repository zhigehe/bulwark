Denmark's current account surplus surged to another peak of DKK 50.7 billion in December 2024 from a downwardly revised DKK 39.5 billion in the previous month.

The goods account surplus increased to DKK 37.2 billion from DKK 25.7 billion in November.], as exports jumped by 9.3% while imports fell by 2.3%.

Also, the services account surplus widened to DKK 8.6 billion from the prior DKK 7.8 billion in November.

At the same time, the secondary income shortfall stood DKK 3.2 billion, little changed from a deficit of DKK 3.3 billion previously.

Meanwhile, the primary income surplus decreased to DKK 8.1 billion from DKK 9.3 billion a month earlier.
