SARAJEVO (Bosnia and Herzegovina), February 11 (SeeNews) - AJet, the low-cost subsidiary of Turkish Airlines, said it will offer flights from Istanbul to Bosnia’s Mostar starting June 4.

The flights on the route will be operated every Wednesday, the AJet said in a press release on Monday.

The seasonal service will be active until October 22, Mostar International Airport said in a Facebook post on Monday.

The Turkish carrier currently offers flights from Istanbul to Bosnia’s Tuzla.

Mostar Airport is one of Bosnia and Herzegovina’s four international airports. The other three are located in Sarajevo, Tuzla and Banja Luka.

The city of Mostar has earmarked 37 million marka ($19.5 million/18.9 million euro) for increasing the capacity of Mostar airport and introducing new routes in its development strategy for 2022-2027.

(1 euro = 1.956 marka)
