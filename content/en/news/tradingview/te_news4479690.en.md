The Netherlands' economy expanded by 0.9% in 2024, up from a modest 0.1% growth in 2023, according to preliminary estimates.

Government consumption was the primary driver, rising by 3.1%.

Household consumption also increased, growing by 1.1%.

The trade balance made a positive contribution, with exports rising by 0.4%—driven by growth in services despite a decline in goods exports—while imports edged up by 0.1%.

Investment contributed slightly negatively to overall growth.

Among sectors, healthcare, business services, and trade played the most significant roles in boosting the economy in 2024.
