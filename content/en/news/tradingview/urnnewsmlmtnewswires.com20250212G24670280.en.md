New Zealand's retail spending via electronic cards fell 1.6% on a seasonally adjusted basis to NZ$6.51 billion in January following a 2.4% monthly increase in December 2024, Stats NZ data showed Thursday.

Core retail spending was down 1.5% to NZ$5.80 billion following a 1.9% rise.

Fuel spending went up 0.2% to NZ$519 million, slower than the 3.7% growth in the prior month.

Services spending growth also eased to 1.8% from 2.1%. Excluding services, spending in non-retail industries rose 1.6% to NZ$2.34 billion, following a 1.2% increase.

Total electronic card spending in January was flat at NZ$9.24 billion.
