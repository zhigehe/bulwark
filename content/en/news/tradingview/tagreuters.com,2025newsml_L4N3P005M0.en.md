The following are the top stories on the business pages of British newspapers. Reuters has not verified these stories and does not vouch for their accuracy.

The Times

- Airlines, including British Airways and Virgin Atlantic, are urging the Civil Aviation Authority to review Heathrow's costs before building a third runway, fearing increased passenger charges.

- AstraZeneca will continue paying Russian doctors and healthcare organizations despite the Ukraine war, citing the need to provide medicines globally and stating that medicines are not subject to sanctions.

The Guardian

- Britain's state-owned Great British Energy faces a challenging search for a CEO as it begins recruiting this month, with no clear frontrunners six months after its 8.3 billion pounds ($10.28 billion) establishment.

- Marks & Spencer's CEO, Stuart Machin, urges the UK government to delay or ease planned tax and recycling charges, claiming the retail industry is being unfairly burdened.

The Telegraph

- Activist investor Elliott has built a stake in BP , aiming to push for strategic changes as the oil giant struggles with its net-zero approach.

- Virgin Atlantic has tripled applications for its loyalty scheme's status-match offer after British Airways announced stricter terms for its frequent flyer programme, shifting to a spending-based system from April.

Sky News

- UK Chancellor Rachel Reeves has summoned executives from Barclays , HSBC , Lloyds , NatWest , and Nationwide for talks on Wednesday to discuss Labour's financial services growth strategy.
