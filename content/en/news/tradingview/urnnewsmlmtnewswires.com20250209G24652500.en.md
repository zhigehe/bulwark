Saudi Arabian Mining (SASE:1211), d/b/a Ma'aden, completed the planned issuance of US dollar-denominated trust certificates worth $1.25 billion in aggregate.

The Saudi Arabian mining group raised $750 million from a five-year tranche at a 5.25% return, according to a Sunday release. A second tranche of $500 million features a 5.5% return and 10-year maturity.

The settlement of the issuance is expected on Thursday. The trust certificates will be listed on the London stock exchange's international securities market.
