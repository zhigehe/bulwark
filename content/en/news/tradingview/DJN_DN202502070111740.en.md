Shares of energy companies ticked up as oil futures recouped some of their recent losses.

Oil futures topped $71 a barrel, bouncing off the psychologically significant $70-per-barrel level, but retreated for a third straight week amid rising U.S. inventories and worries that the Trump administration's higher tariffs on China will dent economic growth and demand for crude.
