Wall Street futures pointed largely sideways pre-bell Thursday, as traders digested the earnings season, and the view for less accommodation from the Federal Reserve, after the relatively "hot" January inflation report Wednesday from Washington.

In the futures, the S&P 500 was steady, the Nasdaq inclined 0.1% and the Dow Jones was flat.

Asian exchanges traded unevenly overnight, while European bourses tracked moderately higher midday on the continent.

AppLovin (APP) rose 30% pre-bell after the mobile software outfit reported strong Q4 results and upbeat guidance, late Wednesday.

MGM Resorts International (MGM) rose 9.2% pre-bell after the hotel-gaming house reported Q4 earnings above expectations, after-bell Wednesday.

Sony (SONY), Moody's (MCO) and Duke Energy (DUK) plan to report earnings pre-bell, among others.

On the economic calendar is the weekly jobless claims bulletin at 8:30 am ET, along with the producer price index (PPI) for January.

The weekly EIA natural gas supplies report posts at 10:30 am.

In premarket action, Bitcoin traded at $96,221, West Texas Intermediate crude oil traded lower at $70.26, and 10-year US Treasuries offered 4.60%. Spot gold traded for $2,917 an ounce.
