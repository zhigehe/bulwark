* Activist hedge fund Elliott Management has built up a near 5% shareholding in oil major BP and wants the company to reduce spending on renewables and make big disposals, the Financial Times reports, citing unnamed sources.
* The U.S. hedge fund is prepared to be a long-term shareholder in BP, the FT reports, adding that its investment could include shares as well as derivative positions.
* Earlier this week The Wall Street Journal and other media reported that the U.S. hedge fund had built an undisclosed stake in BP and wanted to push for transformational changes to improve the company's performance.
* BP subsequently said it would reset its strategy with a new direction to be announced at its capital markets day on Feb. 26.
