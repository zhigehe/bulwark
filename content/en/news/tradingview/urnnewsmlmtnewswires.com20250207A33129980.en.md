US consumer credit use surged by $40.8 billion in December, compared with a $14.6 billion increase expected in a survey compiled by Bloomberg and following a $5.4 billion decrease in the previous month.

Revolving credit use rose by $22.9 billion in December after falling by $13.9 billion in November while nonrevolving credit, which includes purchases of vehicles, rose by $18 billion after an $8.5 billion increase in the previous month.
