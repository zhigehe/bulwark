South Korea's money supply increased in December 2024, with the broad money, or M2, rising 1% to 4,183.5 trillion won from 4,143.0 trillion won a month earlier, the Bank of Korea said Thursday.

M2 is a gauge of the money supply that encompasses cash, demand deposits, and other readily convertible financial instruments.

The country's narrow money, or M1, increased 2% to 1,274.3 trillion won in the month from 1,248.9 trillion won in November 2024.
