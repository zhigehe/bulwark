Chinese shares ended lower, with declines led by consumer and property stocks. Consumer sentiment remained relatively weak despite China's cabinet pledges to boost spending and attract foreign investment. Poly Developments & Holdings Group dropped 2.1% and China Vanke was down 2.4%. Yonghui Superstores slid 3.05% and Yifeng Pharmacy Chain shed 1.7%. Semiconductor Manufacturing International Corp. ended 2.9% lower ahead of its 4Q results. Oil stocks broadly rose, with Cnooc up 1.7% and PetroChina rising 0.7%. The benchmark Shanghai Composite Index ended 0.1% lower at 3318.06, the Shenzhen Composite Index put off 0.5% and the ChiNext Price Index fell 1.4%. (sherry.qin@wsj.com)
