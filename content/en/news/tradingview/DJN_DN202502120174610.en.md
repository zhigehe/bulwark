New Zealand's NZX-50 closed 0.1% lower at 12905.98 after fluctuating between narrow gains and losses for a second consecutive session. The benchmark index traded within a 65-point range on Thursday, failing to establish momentum in either direction following a soft lead from U.S. equities. Large-cap stocks were mixed, with Meridian Energy gaining 0.8%. Medical-device maker Fisher & Paykel Healthcare and infrastructure investor Infratil were flat. Auckland International Airport and power provider Mercury NZ dropped 1.2% and 2.9%, respectively. The NZX-50 is flat for the week. (stuart.condie@wsj.com)
