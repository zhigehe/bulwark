The chronic problems besetting eurozone industry aren't likely to be cured anytime soon, Capital Economics' Elias Hilmer says.Production dropped by 1.1% in December, more than economists had estimated, according to figures released Thursday. And there is "little scope for output to turn a corner in the coming months," Hilmer warns, noting weakness in surveys of the sector."Production will remain subdued in the coming months an the structural problems facing the ailing sector are unlikely to fade soon," he says.
