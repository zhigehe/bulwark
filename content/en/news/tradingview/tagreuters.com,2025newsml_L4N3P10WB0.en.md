Sri Lankan shares closed lower on Monday, dragged down by losses in information technology and consumer discretionary stocks.

\* The CSE All-Share index (.CSE) settled 1.01% lower at 16,566.27. It lost 2.39% last week.

\* UB Finance and Laxapana were the top percentage losers on the index, down 12.5% and 10.7%, respectively.

\* Trading volume on the index rose marginally to 78.6 million shares from 78 million shares in the previous session.

\* The equity market's turnover inched up to 2.07 billion Sri Lankan rupees ($7 million) from 2.06 billion rupees in the previous session, according to exchange data.

\* Foreign investors were net sellers, offloading stocks worth 109.7 million rupees, while domestic investors were net buyers, purchasing nearly 2 billion rupees worth of shares, the data showed.
