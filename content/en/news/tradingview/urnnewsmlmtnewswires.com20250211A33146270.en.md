Asian equities traded in the US as American depositary receipts were trending slightly lower Tuesday morning, declining 0.17% to 2,188.51 on the S&P Asia 50 ADR Index.

From North Asia, the gainers were led by semiconductor company Himax Technologies and e-commerce company Alibaba Group , which rose 4% and 2.3% respectively. They were followed by pet-focused platform Boqii and advertising agency iClick Interactive Asia Group , which were up 2% and 1.7% respectively.

The decliners from North Asia were led by online education company 51Talk Online Education Group and media company Phoenix New Media , which fell 7.5% and 5.5% respectively. They were followed by mobile app developer Cheetah Mobile and automotive e-commerce platform Cango , which were down 5.1% and 4.8% respectively.

From South Asia, the gainers were led by IT firm Sify Technologies , which increased 1.2%, followed by financial services company ICICI Bank and IT firm Infosys , which were up 0.8% and 0.1% respectively.

The decliners from South Asia were led by telecommunications operator Telekomunikasi Indonesia and IT firm Wipro , which dropped 1.8% and 0.9% respectively. They were followed by telecommunications operator PLDT and pharmaceutical company Dr. Reddy's Laboratories , which were off 0.8% and 0.5% respectively.
