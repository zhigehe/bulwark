Spending on ANZ-issued cards fell 0.9% on an annual basis in January following a 0.6% drop in December 2024, data from ANZ Research showed Thursday.

Industries that are most sensitive to interest rates continued to experience the biggest declines. Tourism was the biggest loser with a 9.5% fall in spending. Durables and clothing spending declined 6.6% and 5.9%, respectively, while housing and discretionary spending fell 5.5% and 1.5%, respectively.

In contrast, utilities and repairs rose 4.3%, followed by business goods and services, which increased 3.1%. Miscellaneous services and goods retail spending grew 2.2% and 1.2%, respectively.

On a monthly basis, credit card spending via ANZ-issued cards rose 0.6% in January.
