The annual inflation rate in Costa Rica rose by 1.14% in January 2025, following a 0.95% increase in the previous month, marking the fourth increase in the last twenty months and the fastest in the series.

Prices accelerated for food and beverages (6.06% vs. 2.60% in December), education (3.73 vs. 1.52%), restaurants and hotels (3.09% vs. 2.81%) and health (1.61% vs. 1.56%), while the pace of decline slowed for transport (-2.75% vs. -4.48%).

In contrast, price growth reversed for communications (-2.46% vs. 0.66%), while it eased for housing and utilities (1.20% vs. 4.91%).

On a monthly basis, consumer prices rose by 0.36%, following a 0.94% increase in the prior month.
