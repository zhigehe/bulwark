Portugal's consumer price inflation rate fell to 2.5% in January 2025 from 3.0% in December, driven primarily by a slowdown in the cost of food and non-alcoholic beverages (1.4% vs. 3.4%) and housing and utilities (3.5% vs. 7.1%).

Inflation also eased slightly for transportation (2.4% vs. 2.5%) and miscellaneous goods and services (1.9% vs. 2.0%).

However, price increases accelerated in recreation and culture (4.0% vs. 2.5%) and restaurants and hotels (5.6% vs. 5.2%).

Meanwhile, core inflation, which excludes volatile items like unprocessed food and energy, edged down to 2.7% from 2.8%.

On a monthly basis, consumer prices dropped by 0.5% in January, reversing a 0.1% rise in December.
