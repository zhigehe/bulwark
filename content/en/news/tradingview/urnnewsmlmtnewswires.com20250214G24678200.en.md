Spain's annual inflation rate rose to 2.9% in January from 2.8% in the previous month, final data from the National Statistics Institute showed Friday.

The reading is below the flash estimate of a 3% rate.

On a monthly basis, consumer prices in Spain edged up 0.2%, in line with the preliminary reading.

The annual harmonized inflation rate was confirmed at 2.9%, against the prior 2.8%. Month over month, harmonized consumer prices fell 0.1%, consistent with the flash estimate.

Meanwhile, the core inflation rate fell to 2.4% from 2.6%.
