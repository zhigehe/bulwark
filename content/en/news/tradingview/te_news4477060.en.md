Hong Kong's equities surged 283 points, or 1.3%, to 22,140 in early trading on Thursday, marking the second consecutive day of gains and reaching a four-month high.

Most sectors advanced, buoyed by optimism over a potential peace deal between Ukraine and Russia following phone calls between US President Trump, Russian President Putin, and Ukrainian President Zelenskiy.
