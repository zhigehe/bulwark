New Zealand's NZX-50 index ended 0.3% higher at 12917.45, led by Vulcan Steel which pointed to an improving market for its products this year after a challenging run. Vulcan Steel rose 4.0% after projecting a recovery in trading volumes in New Zealand from as soon as 2Q. Shares in insurer Tower hit their highest level since early 2016, up 3.6%, after reporting continued growth in gross written premium in 1Q. Power generators were broadly higher, with Meridian Energy up 1.3% and Genesis Energy advancing 0.7%. However, Manawa Energy declined by 0.4%. (david.winning@wsj.com; @dwinningWSJ)
