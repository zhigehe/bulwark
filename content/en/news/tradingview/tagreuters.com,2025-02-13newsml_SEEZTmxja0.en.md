BELGRADE (Serbia), February 13 (SeeNews) - The blue-chip index of the Belgrade Stock Exchange, the BELEX15, rose on Thursday, lifted by gains in industrial gases producer Messer Tehnogas [BEL:TGAS], bourse data showed.

The BELEX15 advanced by 1.22% to close at 1,128.06 points.

Messer, the only blue-chip gainer on Thursday, rose by 7.93%, closing at 34,000 dinars as 51 shares traded. Messer generated the largest single turnover of 1.7 million dinars for the day.

Cookware manufacturer Metalac [BEL:MTLC] was the only blue-chip decliner, falling by 1.25% to 1,975 dinars at close in a trade of 52 shares.

Aerodrom Nikola Tesla [BEL:AERO] closed unchanged at 1,999 dinars as 135 shares changed hands. Civil engineering company Energoprojekt Holding [BEL:ENHL] also closed flat, at 401 dinars, as two shares traded.

No other blue-chip companies traded on Thursday.

The broader BELEXline, a free-float market capitalisation-weighted index, also rose, by 1.26%, to 2,487.13 points at close.

Total turnover on the Belgrade bourse declined to 5.2 million dinars ($46,125/ 44,405 euro) from 15.3 million dinars on Wednesday. Trading in government securities accounted for some 1.2 million dinars of the total on Thursday, compared to 12.6 million dinars on Wednesday.

(1 euro = 117.108 dinars)
