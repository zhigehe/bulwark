Suvo Strategic Minerals extended its take-or-pay offtake agreement with Chinese firm Chaozhou Chengcheng Industrial Co. until February 2030, according to a Friday filing with the Australian bourse.

The agreement, valued at roughly AU$6.6 million, now covers a minimum order quantity of 8,750 tonnes of hydrous kaolin over a five-year period, up from the previous three-year deal covering 4,275 tonnes, the filing said.

The extended agreement includes a 25% annual increase in minimum order volumes and will start in February, according to the filing.

Suvo Strategic Minerals shares rose 6% in afternoon trade Friday.
