STOCKS:

The benchmark BSE Sensex was down 0.4% at 77,510, while the broader NSE Nifty 50 declined 0.5% to 23447, as global trade concerns weighed after U.S. President Donald Trump's warning that he would impose new tariffs this week.

RUPEE:

The Indian rupee dropped 0.5% to 87.8625 per U.S. dollar, tracking a decline in regional peers spurred by the risk of US trade tariffs.

GOVERNMENT BONDS:

The benchmark 10-year bond (IN067934G=CC) was quoted at 100.435 rupees, with the yield up 2 bps at 6.7263%, as traders remain cautious after the central bank's policy decision and look out for choice of papers for bond purchases this week.

OVERNIGHT INDEX SWAPS:

The one-year overnight index swap rate (INR1YMIBROIS=CC) was up 2 basis points at 6.3675%, while the five-year swap rate (INR5YMIBROIS=CC) rose nearly 4 bps to 6.1525%.

CALL MONEY:

India's overnight call money rate (INROND=) was at 6.40%, and the overnight TREPS rate (INTREPTOT=TCCL) was at 6.32%
