Crude palm oil ended higher, rallying above MYR4600 on bullish sentiment after the Malaysian Palm Oil Board report, which showed another month of decline in stock levels in the country, according to David Ng, a trader at Kuala Lumpur-based Iceberg X. Weaker-than-expected output growth also maintained the positive sentiment in the market, he adds. Ng sees support at MYR4,500 and resistance at MYR4,700. The Bursa Malaysia Derivatives contract for April delivery rose MYR91 to MYR4,595 a ton. (tracy.qu@wsj.com)
