Saudi Arabia's Capital Markets Authority said Thursday three individuals were convicted for breaches of capital market law and other related regulations.

The Appeal Committee for Resolution of Securities Disputes made two of the decisions, while the Committee for the Resolution of Securities Disputes passed the other.

A total penalty of 500,000 Saudi riyals was imposed against Osama Wafiq Tawfiq As'ad, Bader bin Abdullah bin Hamad Alhammad and Muhammad bin Mahdi bin Saleh Aldraibi Alzahrani for the violations involving material information disclosure, capital market institutions regulations and securities business licenses.
