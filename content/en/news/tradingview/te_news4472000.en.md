Industrial production in Argentina surged by 8.4% year-on-year in December 2024, breaking an eighteen-month contraction streak and rebounding from a 1.7% drop in November.

Most major sectors swung into expansion, including machinery and equipment (17.7% vs. -1.9% in November), oil refining, coke, and nuclear fuel (4.1% vs. -4.5%), wood, paper, publishing, and printing (-0.3% vs. -7.6%), and manufacturing (33.4% vs. -3.1%), while equipment, devices, and instruments (68.1% vs. 3.4%) surged, and growth remained stable in food and beverages (8.1% vs. 8.2%).

Meanwhile, basic metals (-4.9% vs. -12.6%) was the only sector in contraction, although at a slower pace.

On a seasonally adjusted monthly basis, industrial production increased by 0.2%, extending the recovery from a 0.4% increase in the prior month.
