\*\* India will be among the most impacted from a potential U.S. reciprocal tariff, brokerages say

\*\* U.S. President Donald Trump says he will announce plans to impose reciprocal tariffs on several countries in the next two days

\*\* Nomura says India and Thailand have higher tariff rates on U.S. exports and are thus at the risk of higher reciprocal tariffs

\*\* Morgn Stanley and Citi says tariff differentials most pronounced for India, making it vulnerable to a 4-6 percentage points hike

\*\* There may be scope for India to increase purchases in defence equipment, energy and aircraft to narrow trade surplus with U.S., three brokerages add

\*\* India's Prime Minister Narendra Modi is preparing additional tariff cuts ahead of a meeting with Trump this week to avoid a potential trade war
