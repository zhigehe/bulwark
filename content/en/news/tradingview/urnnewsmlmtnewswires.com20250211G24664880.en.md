South Korea's four major banks - KB Financial Group's KB Kookmin, Shinhan Financial Group's Shinhan Bank, Woori Financial Group's Woori Bank, and NH Nonghyup - will close 170 offline branches by March, citing increased mobile banking demand.

Shinhan and Woori will each cut over 50 branches, while NH Nonghyup and KB Kookmin will reduce 36 and 25, respectively, Yonhap News reported Tuesday.

In contrast, Hana Financial Group's Hana Bank will expand its branch network to 602 from 597, it said.

Shares of Woori Financial Group rose nearly 3% in recent trade on Wednesday while those of Hana Financial Group increased nearly 1%.
