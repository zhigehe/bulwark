ZAGREB (Croatia), February 12 (SeeNews) - The main share indices of the Zagreb Stock Exchange recouped on Wednesday on gains in hotel owner and manager Valamar [ZSE:RIVP] and Hrvatski Telekom [ZSE:HT], bourse data showed.

The bourse’s 19-share equity benchmark, the CROBEX, rose 0.51% to 3,456.57 points.

The narrower blue-chip CROBEX10 index added 0.22% to 2,177.63 points.

Index heavyweight electrical concern Koncar [ZSE:KOEI] was the most traded company with a turnover of 283,000 euro ($295,000) in regular trading. Its shares dropped 1.89% to 520 euro and were the biggest daily decliner among the blue chips. However, they appreciated 143% in the last one year.

Valamar led the blue-chip gainers with its shares ending 2.45% higher, at 6.68 euro in a daily turnover of 210,000 euro.

Shares of Hrvatski Telekom went up 0.94% to 43 euro in a turnover of 208,000 euro.

The bourse's regular turnover reached 2.5 million euro from 2.0 million euro a day earlier.

Besides, the bourse registered a block deal of some 605,000 euro in which 1,170 shares of Koncar changed hands at an average price of 517.20 euro apiece.

($ = 0.960 euro)
