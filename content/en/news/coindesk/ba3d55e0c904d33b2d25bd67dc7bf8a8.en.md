Popular trading platform Robinhood (HOOD) beat its fourth-quarter earnings and revenue estimates, which could have positive read-through for peer Coinbase (COIN).

Robinhood said its fourth-quarter revenue rose 115% from the previous year to $1.01 billion, beating Wall Street analysts' estimate of $945.8 million, according to FactSet data. Its transaction-based revenue rose 200% from the previous year, mainly due to a 700% rise in cryptocurrency revenue, the firm said in a [statement](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1783879/000178387925000045/q42024robinhoodexhibit991.htm).

Robinhood also reported earnings per share of $1.01, topping the average estimate of $0.42.

HOOD's shares were up more than 5% in post-market trading on Wednesday, while COIN's stock was up about 0.3%.
