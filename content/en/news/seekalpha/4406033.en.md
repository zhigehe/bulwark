The Travelers Companies (NYSE:[TRV](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/TRV "The Travelers Companies, Inc.")) expects catastrophe losses of $1.7 billion pre-tax ($1.3 billion after-tax) related to the January Los Angeles wildfires.

The preliminary estimate includes losses from the company’s personal and commercial segments, including its Fidelis quota share, as well as estimated assessments from the California FAIR Plan and recoveries from reinsurance.

JPMorgan analysts previously noted that Travelers (NYSE:[TRV](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/TRV "The Travelers Companies, Inc.")), Allstate ([ALL](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/ALL "The Allstate Corporation")) and Chubb ([CB](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/CB "Chubb Limited")) are likely to be the most exposed to insured losses from the wildfires.

Chubb ([CB](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/CB "Chubb Limited")) in its recent [earnings call](https://seekingalpha.com/article/4752994-chubb-cb-q4-2024-earnings-call-transcript "earnings call") said it estimates catastrophe loss of $1.5B net pre-tax from LA wildfires.
