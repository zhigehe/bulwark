GameStop (NYSE:[GME](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/GME "GameStop Corp.")) stock soared nearly 10% on Monday amid online chatter that the video game retailer may be weighing a strategy around bitcoin ([BTC-USD](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/BTC-USD "Bitcoin USD")), sparked by its CEO Ryan Cohen posting a photo with Strategy ([MSTR](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/MSTR "Strategy")) co-founder Michael Saylor.

Strategy ([MSTR](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/MSTR "Strategy")) is the largest corporate holder of bitcoin ([BTC-USD](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/BTC-USD "Bitcoin USD")), and Saylor has long touted the benefits of holding the top cryptocurrency as a store of value.

Cohen posted the [photo](https://x.com/ryancohen/status/1888098643732811864 "photo") on social media platform X over the weekend, with no caption or details regarding the meeting.

But it was enough to fuel speculation over GameStop (NYSE:[GME](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/GME "GameStop Corp.")) potentially eyeing a new strategy, more than a year after it ended support for its crypto and NFT wallet due to ["regulatory uncertainty."](https://seekingalpha.com/news/3994551-gamestop-to-stop-supporting-crypto-wallets-citing-regulatory-uncertainty "\"regulatory uncertainty.\"")

The meme stock gained ended 9.7% higher at $27.12/share on Monday, its biggest intraday percentage gain in over three months.

Strategy ([MSTR](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/MSTR "Strategy")), [formerly MicroStrategy](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4403831-microstrategy-changes-name-to-strategy "formerly MicroStrategy"), closed up 2.2% at $334.62/share. The company currently holds 478,740 BTC, valued at around $47.13B.
