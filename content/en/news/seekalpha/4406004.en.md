Archer Aviation Inc. (NYSE:[ACHR](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/ACHR "Archer Aviation Inc.")) announced on Tuesday that it has raised $300 million. Blackrock ([BLK](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/BLK "BlackRock, Inc.")) was one of the leading institutional investors that participated in the financing round.

The new investment is seen further reinforcing the company's strong financial position and strategically positioning it to accelerate the development of its hybrid aircraft platform for the defense market and beyond. 

The new investment raise brings Archer's (NYSE:[ACHR](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/ACHR "Archer Aviation Inc.")) total liquidity position to ~$1 billion. The additional capital is seen further strengthening its position.

Archer Aviation ([ACHR](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/ACHR "Archer Aviation Inc.")) also released certain preliminary estimated financial results for Q4, reporting that its GAAP operating expenses will be within the range of $120 million and $140 million and total non-GAAP operating expenses are in line with its guidance range of $95 million to $110 million. The San Jose-based company also confirmed that it does not expect that its total non-GAAP operating expenses for the first quarter of 2025 will materially increase over the guided range.

Shares of Archer Aviation ([ACHR](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/ACHR "Archer Aviation Inc.")) fell 2.68% in premarket trading on Tuesday.
