Moody's (NYSE:[MCO](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/MCO "Moody's Corporation")) stock gained 2.9% in Thursday morning trading after delivering stronger-than-expected 2025 guidance for earnings, although the company baked in markedly slower growth relative to the prior year. At the same time, Q4 earnings came in mixed as expenses drifted higher during the three-month period.

The provider of credit ratings and market data analytics sees full-year 2025 adjusted EPS of $14.00-$14.50, of which the $14.25 midpoint exceeds the $13.59 average analyst estimate. Revenue for the year is expected to grow in the high-single-digit percent range, compared with +6.05% consensus and +24% in 2024.

It also expects 2025 free cash flow of $2.40B-$2.60B (midpoint $2.50B vs. $2.59B Visible Alpha consensus), and 2025 adjusted operating margin of about 50%.

Meanwhile, [Q4 2024 adjusted EPS](https://seekingalpha.com/pr/20001199-moodys-corporation-reports-results-for-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2024-sets-outlook-for "Q4 adjusted EPS") of $2.62, topping the $2.58 Wall Street consensus, slid from $3.21 in the prior quarter and advanced from $2.19 in Q4 2023.

Revenue of $1.67B in Q4, falling short of the $1.71B expected, fell from $1.81B in Q3 and climbed from $1.48B in the year-ago period.

Expenses totaled $1.11B, up from $1.08B in Q3 and $982M in Q4 2023.

Adjusted operating margin retreated to 43.8% from 47.8% in the earlier quarter and edged up from 42.6% a year before.

Conference [call](https://ir.moodys.com/investor-relations/default.aspx "call") at 11:30 a.m. ET.
