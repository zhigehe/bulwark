Columbus McKinnon (NASDAQ:[CMCO](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/CMCO "Columbus McKinnon Corporation")) +1.4% post-market Monday it [agreeing to acquire](https://seekingalpha.com/pr/19997305-columbus-mckinnon-to-combine-with-kito-crosby-delivering-compelling-value-creation "agreed to acquire") Kito Crosby Ltd. from KKR ([KKR](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/KKR "KKR & Co. Inc.")) for $2.7B in cash, which it said will advance its strategy as the holistic provider of intelligent motion solutions in materials handling.

Kito Crosby, which generated $1.1B in revenues in 2024, is a global leader in lifting solutions with multiple manufacturing assembly plants and nearly 4,000 employees serving over 50 countries; KKR ([KKR](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/KKR "KKR & Co. Inc.")) has owned the company since 2013 and has more than doubled revenue during the period.

Columbus McKinon (NASDAQ:[CMCO](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/CMCO "Columbus McKinnon Corporation")) said it expects the deal will create ~$70M in annual net cost synergies, improving adjusted EBITDA margins to greater than 23% while more than doubling revenue and tripling adjusted EBITDA on a pro-forma combined basis.

As a result of CD&R's investment in Columbus McKinnon ([CMCO](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/CMCO "Columbus McKinnon Corporation")), it is expected that Mike Lamach, Nate Sleeper and Andrew Campelli will join the company's board of directors when the acqusition closes.
