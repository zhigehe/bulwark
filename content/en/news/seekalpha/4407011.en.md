DraftKings (NASDAQ:[DKNG](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/DKNG "DraftKings Inc.")) appears to be one of the big winners of Super Bowl LIX. The online sports betting giant benefited on the moneyline and with futures bets from the Philadelphia Eagles winning. Key prop and parlays bets also mostly went the house's way, particularly with Saquon Barkley and Travis Kelce not scoring a touchdown.

Bank of America analyst Shaun Kelley thinks a big jump in Super Bowl handle for DraftKings (NASDAQ:[DKNG](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/DKNG "DraftKings Inc.")) and the strong hold rate could add as much as $25 million in additional adjusted EBITDA for the Boston-based company. For reference, Draftkings ([DKNG](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/DKNG "DraftKings Inc.")) generated total adjusted EBITDA of just $22.4 million a year ago in Q1.

In addition, DraftKings ([DKNG](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/DKNG "DraftKings Inc.")) led active user share on Sunday with 23%, followed by FanDuel at 17%, and Bet365, PrizePicks, and ESPN Bet. DraftKings ([DKNG](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/DKNG "DraftKings Inc.")) also led overall downloads and reached #3 in the overall app store on Sunday.

Looking ahead, the big question for DraftKings ([DKNG](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/DKNG "DraftKings Inc.")) will be if the company raises guidance alongside its earnings report, given the solid start to 2025. On that note, BofA cautioned that new potential tax headwinds in Maryland and Ohio, as well as launch costs in Missouri, could keep the financial outlook on the conservative side.

Shares of DraftKings ([DKNG](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/DKNG "DraftKings Inc.")) are up more than 5% since the Super Bowl and are up more than 20% on a year-to-date basis.
