Earnings Call Insights: Q2 Holdings (NYSE:[QTWO](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/QTWO "Q2 Holdings, Inc.")) Q4 2024

### Management View

* CEO Matt Flake highlighted Q4 2024 as the company’s second-strongest bookings quarter ever, with balanced contributions across net new wins and expansion deals. The quarter also marked the best renewal performance in company history, with 10% of the digital banking customer base renewing.
* Flake emphasized the strength of the company’s platform, citing its ability to secure seven Tier 1 and enterprise deals in Q4, as well as its increasing focus on cross-selling opportunities within its existing customer base.
* CFO Jonathan Price noted that subscription revenue now represents 79% of total revenue and highlighted operational efficiency gains and increased free cash flow generation as key financial achievements.

### Outlook

* Management projects full-year 2025 revenue of $772M to $779M, representing 11-12% year-over-year growth. Adjusted EBITDA for 2025 is expected to be between $165M and $170M, with margins rising to 21-22%.
* Subscription revenue growth is forecasted to reach approximately 15% in 2025, with an adjusted subscription growth target of 13% for 2026.
* The company updated its three-year framework, raising its average annual subscription revenue growth target to 15% and increasing its free cash flow conversion target for 2026 to over 85%.

### Financial Results

* Q4 2024 revenue was $183M, up 13% year-over-year and exceeding analysts’ revenue estimate of $179.95M. Adjusted EBITDA for the quarter reached $37.6M, representing 20.6% of revenue.
* Total ARR rose to $824M, up 12% year-over-year, while subscription ARR increased by 15% to $682M.
* Gross margins for the quarter improved to 57.4%, driven by a higher mix of subscription-based revenues and operational efficiencies.

### Q&A

* Dan Perlin, RBC Capital Markets: Questioned whether larger deal sizes are contributing to sequential backlog growth. CFO Price responded that the primary driver in Q4 was renewal activity, which accounted for 10% of the digital banking customer base.
* Alan Hodgkiss, Goldman Sachs: Asked about barriers to cross-selling commercial solutions. CEO Flake cited the complexity of transitioning crown-jewel assets but emphasized Q2’s proven track record and operational discipline as key differentiators.
* Parker Lane, Stifel: Inquired about the doubling of Innovation Studio bookings. CFO Price noted that growth is primarily driven by increased adoption among existing customers and expanded use cases within their ecosystems.

### Sentiment Analysis

* Analysts maintained a positive tone, focusing on Q2 Holdings’ strong renewal activity, cross-sell opportunities, and operational efficiency improvements. Skepticism was minimal, with most questions aimed at understanding the drivers of growth.
* Management displayed confidence, particularly in their ability to capitalize on cross-selling opportunities and maintain strong renewal rates. Flake emphasized the company’s competitive differentiation and long-term customer relationships.

### Quarter-over-Quarter Comparison

* Subscription ARR growth in Q4 2024 reached 15%, up from 14% in Q3, reflecting continued strength in bookings and renewals.
* Gross margins improved to 57.4% in Q4 from 56% in Q3, driven by an increasing subscription revenue mix and operational efficiencies.
* Backlog growth accelerated in Q4 to $189M sequentially, compared to $78M in Q3, primarily due to strong renewal activity.

### Risks and Concerns

* Management acknowledged ongoing declines in professional services revenue, which fell 11% year-over-year in 2024. This trend is expected to persist as the company prioritizes higher-margin subscription revenue.
* Analysts raised questions about the competitive landscape, particularly in cross-selling commercial solutions and the potential impact of deregulation on IT spending priorities.

### Final Takeaway

Q2 Holdings delivered strong Q4 2024 results, highlighted by record-breaking renewal activity and robust subscription revenue growth. The company is well-positioned for 2025, with a focus on expanding its share within existing customers and executing on its profitable growth strategy. Management’s confidence in achieving 15% subscription revenue growth and increased free cash flow conversion underscores their long-term strategic priorities.
