China Vanke ([OTCPK:CHVKF](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/CHVKF "China Vanke Co., Ltd.")) has secured a 2.8 billion yuan ($383.12 million) loan from its major shareholder, state-owned Shenzhen Metro to repay debt, indicating further support from the government for the cash-strapped developer.

Vanke, in return, will pledge 211.5 million shares, or 18.3%, of its listed property services unit, Onewo Inc, as collateral, the company said in a [filing late on Monday.](https://www1.hkexnews.hk/listedco/listconews/sehk/2025/0210/2025021000901.pdf " filing late on Monday.")

As of the date of the announcement, Shenzhen Metro Group held about 27.18% of the total issued share capital of China Vanke ([OTCPK:CHVKF](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/CHVKF "China Vanke Co., Ltd.")).

In a separate filing on Monday, Vanke published a repayment announcement for its 3 billion yuan notes maturing on February 16, signalling that it will make the repayment on time.

Earlier this year, China Vanke's chairman Yu Liang and CEO Zhu Jiusheng stepped down as it forecast a record $6.2 billion net loss for 2024.
