The federal government spent $126.6B more than it collected in January, exceeding the $89.9B consensus estimate, according to the U.S. Treasury's monthly statement released on Wednesday.

The budget gap swelled from a $86.7B deficit in December and a $21.9B shortfall in January 2024. 

Outlays in January totaled $641.9B, up from $541.2B in December, while receipts increased to $513.3B from $454.4B, the U.S. Treasury [said.](https://fiscaldata.treasury.gov/static-data/published-reports/mts/MonthlyTreasuryStatement_202501.pdf " said.")

For the government's fiscal year-to-date, the deficit stands at $839.6B, vs. $531.9B in the same period a year ago. The federal government's fiscal year ends on Sept. 30.
