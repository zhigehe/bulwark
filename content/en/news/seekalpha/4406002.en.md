Shopify (NYSE:[SHOP](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SHOP "Shopify Inc.")) reported revenue shot up 31.3% year-over-year in Q4 to $2.81 billion to accelerate from the 24% growth seen for the same quarter last year. Q4 marked the e-commerce company's seventh consecutive quarter of 25% or greater revenue growth when excluding logistics.

Gross merchandise volume rose 26% to $94.46 billion to beat the consensus estimate of $93.01 billion. Monthly recurring revenue was up 24% to $178 million during the quarter, vs. the consensus estimate of $182.4 million. Operating income was up 61% to $465 million, and free cash flow grew 37% to $611 million.

"We grew free cash flow margin sequentially each quarter of 2024, reaching 22% for Q4. GMV growth accelerated each quarter this year, achieving a 24% year-over-year increase in 2024, marking our highest GMV growth in three years. These consistent results are a testament to our strategic initiatives and operational discipline, positioning us well for continued success and growth in the future," highlighted CFO Jeff Hoffmeister.

Looking ahead, Shopify (NYSE:[SHOP](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SHOP "Shopify Inc.")) sees Q1 revenue growing at a mid-twenties percentage rate on a year-over-year basis. Gross profit dollars are expected to grow at a low-twenties percentage rate. Operating expense as a percentage of revenue are forecast to be 41% to 42%.

Shares of Shopify ([SHOP](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SHOP "Shopify Inc.")) were down 7.66% in premarket trading on Tuesday.
