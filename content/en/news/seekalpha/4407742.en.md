Sibanye Stillwater (NYSE:[SBSW](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SBSW "Sibanye Stillwater Limited")) -6.4% in early trading Thursday after announcing founding CEO Neal Froneman will [retire](https://www.miningmx.com/top-story/59986-sibanye-stillwater-appoints-richard-stewart-as-ceo-designate/ "retire"), to be succeeded by Richard Stewart, currently the company's South Africa chief regional officer.

Froneman will formerly step down from the company in September, while Stewart's appointment as CEO-designate is effective from March 1.

Stewart joined Sibanye Stillwater (NYSE:[SBSW](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SBSW "Sibanye Stillwater Limited")) in 2014, and has been South Africa's chief regional officer from May 2022, after serving as the company's COO since December 2020.

Froneman founded Sibanye Stillwater ([SBSW](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SBSW "Sibanye Stillwater Limited")), then called Sibanye Gold, in 2013 after taking over Gold Fields' South African mines Driefontein and Kloof.
