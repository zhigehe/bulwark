Cemex (NYSE:[CX](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/CX "CEMEX, S.A.B. de C.V.")) -1.3% pre-market Monday after announcing it appointed current Cemex USA President Jaime Muguiro as the company's [new CEO](https://seekingalpha.com/pr/19996304-cemex-announces-ceo-transition "new CEO") effective April 1, succeeding Fernando Gonzalez, who will retire after a 35-year career at the company.

Muguiro joined Cemex (NYSE:[CX](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/CX "CEMEX, S.A.B. de C.V.")) in 1996 and has headed several regional operations for the company, including the Mediterranean, South, Central America and the Caribbean, and most recently serves as President of U.S. operations.

Gonzalez joined Cemex ([CX](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/CX "CEMEX, S.A.B. de C.V.")) in 1989, eventually leading various regions of the company, including SCA&C, Europe, Asia, and Oceania, before becoming CFO in 2011 and finally CEO in 2014.
