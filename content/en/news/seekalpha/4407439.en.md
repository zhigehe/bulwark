The British economy expanded 0.1% in the final quarter of 2024, seeing no growth from the previous quarter, above estimates of a 0.1% contraction, preliminary estimates showed.

On the production side, the services sector increased by 0.2%, construction grew, by 0.5%, while production fell by 0.8%.

On the expenditure side, there were falls in expenditure terms in net trade and gross fixed capital formation that were offset by a large increase in change in inventories.

Monthly GDP in the United Kingdom increased to 0.40% M/M in December from 0.10% in November, a Y/Y surge of 1.5%.
