* The annual inflation rate in Germany was confirmed at 2.3% in January 2025, easing from 2.6% in December and meeting estimates.
* Food inflation slowed significantly to 0.8%, compared to 2.0% in the previous month, while prices for services and goods moderated. Meanwhile, energy costs declined by 1.6%, the same pace as in December, mainly due to drops in household energy and motor fuels. Electricity prices also fell.
* Core inflation, which excludes volatile items such as food and energy, eased to 2.9%, the lowest in three months.
* On a monthly basis, consumer prices fell by 0.2% in January, reversing a 0.5% increase in December.
