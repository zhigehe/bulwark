Ormat Technologies (NYSE:[ORA](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/ORA "Ormat Technologies, Inc.")) +1.1% in early trading Monday after saying it [launched commercial operations](https://seekingalpha.com/pr/19996765-ormat-commences-commercial-operation-of-35-mw-ijen-geothermal-facility-in-indonesia "launched commercial operations") for the 35 MW Ijen geothermal power plant in Indonesia, jointly owned with PT Medco Power Indonesia; Ormat's share of the facility is 17 MW.

The Ijen plant, equipped with Ormat's (NYSE:[ORA](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/ORA "Ormat Technologies, Inc.")) Energy Converter, began operations with its first phase, delivering 35 MW of electricity power to the Java grid, and the Phase 1 commencement marksfa significant step of the Ijen facility, which has a total planned capacity of 110 MW under a 30-year power purchase agreement.

The plant is the first geothermal power plant in East Java, Indonesia, contributing to Indonesia's plan for an additional 7.2 GW of geothermal capacity by 2035.

"The launch of the Ijen facility is a key step in our strategy to consistently and accretively grow our leading global geothermal energy portfolio and expand our presence in Indonesia," Ormat ([ORA](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/ORA "Ormat Technologies, Inc.")) CEO Doron Blachar said.
