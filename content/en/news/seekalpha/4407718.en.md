SunCar Technology Group Inc. (NASDAQ:[SDA](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SDA "SunCar Technology Group Inc.")) announced on Thursday the expansion of a strategic partnership with a prominent electric vehicle manufacturer. The company said the collaboration, which began in early 2024, has experienced exceptional growth in automotive insurance brokerage services.

Over the course of the year, premium volumes increased significantly, from $400,000 in January to approximately $40 million by December. Currently, more than 2 million of the partner's electric vehicles are on the road in China, supported by SunCar's (NASDAQ:[SDA](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SDA "SunCar Technology Group Inc.")) newly developed e-insurance system designed to streamline the insurance renewal process for this expanding market.

Looking forward to 2025, SunCar ([SDA](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SDA "SunCar Technology Group Inc.")) plans to broaden its geographical presence and enhance its technological integration with the electric vehicle manufacturer. A major initiative involves embedding SunCar's ([SDA](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SDA "SunCar Technology Group Inc.")) specialized automotive services directly into the EV manufacturer’s owner platform, creating a seamless experience for millions of vehicle owners. The integration is seen as representing a significant advancement in digital automotive service delivery.

On a broad scale, Ye Zaichang, Chairman and CEO of SunCar ([SDA](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SDA "SunCar Technology Group Inc.")) highlighted the company’s commitment to addressing the evolving needs of vehicle owners through innovative solutions as artificial intelligence continues to reshape the automotive industry. The new expanded partnership is anticipated to solidify SunCar's ([SDA](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SDA "SunCar Technology Group Inc.")) position in China's rapidly growing electric vehicle market and underscores the ability to leverage technology to improve automotive services and insurance solutions.

Shares of SunCar ([SDA](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SDA "SunCar Technology Group Inc.")) were down 1.1% in Thursday morning trading. The stock collapsed last week after a public offering of stock was announced.
