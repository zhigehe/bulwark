British American Tobacco (NYSE:[BTI](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/BTI "British American Tobacco p.l.c.")) shares continued seven straight sessions of gains, as the stock closed 0.52% up at $42.52 on Tuesday.

The London-based tobacco giant rose about 6.44% in the preceding six sessions. The stock closed 1.29% higher at $42.30 on Monday.

BTI’s shares have gained more than 36% in the last one year, outperforming the broader S&P500 index, which rose nearly 21% during the same period.

Looking at Seeking Alpha's Quant Rating, BTI has a [Buy](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/BTI/ratings/quant-ratings) rating with a score of 4.22 out of 5. The company received A+ in the prospect of profitability, A in momentum, while it got C- in the growth factor.

Seeking Alpha analysts also [see](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/BTI/ratings/author-ratings) the stock as a Buy.

“BTI's diverse product portfolio, including combustibles, oral products, heated tobacco, and vapes, positions it well despite the decline in traditional smoking,” [noted](https://seekingalpha.com/article/4752717-british-american-tobacco-our-favorite-consumer-staples-stock-for-2025) Seeking Alpha analyst PropNotes.

The analyst added that BTI aims to become a fully smokeless company by 2035, leveraging growth in non-combustible nicotine products to sustain financial performance.

Turning to the [Wall Street](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/BTI/ratings/sell-side-ratings) community, one analyst gave BTI a Buy. Two analysts have given the stock a Hold recommendation, and one recommended Strong Sell.
