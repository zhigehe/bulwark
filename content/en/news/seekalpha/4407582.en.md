Nissan Motor Co. ([OTCPK:NSANY](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/NSANY "Nissan Motor Co., Ltd.")) and Honda Motor (NYSE:[HMC](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/HMC "Honda Motor Co., Ltd.")) have called off talks on a deal that would have created the world's third-largest carmaker. The development was widely anticipated after discussions had stalled in recent weeks to break off any merger momentum.

Nissan ([OTCPK:NSANY](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/NSANY "Nissan Motor Co., Ltd.")), Honda (NYSE:[HMC](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/HMC "Honda Motor Co., Ltd.")), and Mitsubishi Motors ([OTCPK:MMTOF](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/MMTOF "Mitsubishi Motors Corporation")) formally agreed to terminate a memorandum of understanding.

"Going forward, Nissan and Honda will collaborate within the framework of a strategic partnership aimed at the era of intelligence and electrified vehicles, striving to create new value and maximize the corporate value of both companies," read a statement.

Following the merger announcement, Bloomberg reported that KKR & Co. ([KKR](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/KKR "KKR & Co. Inc.")) is weighing an investment in Nissan Motor ([OTCPK:NSANY](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/NSANY "Nissan Motor Co., Ltd.")) Sources indicated that the private equity firm is in the early stages of evaluating an equity or debt investment to improve Nissan's ([OTCPK:NSANY](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/NSANY "Nissan Motor Co., Ltd.")) financial position.

Shares of Nissan ([OTCPK:NSANY](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/NSANY "Nissan Motor Co., Ltd.")) edged 0.2% higher in premarket trading, while Honda Motor ([HMC](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/HMC "Honda Motor Co., Ltd.")) gained 1.6%. Toyota Motor ([TM](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/TM "Toyota Motor Corporation")) was up 0.7% in the early session.
