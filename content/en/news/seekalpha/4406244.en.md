Kraft Heinz (NASDAQ:[KHC](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/KHC "The Kraft Heinz Company")) is set to report fourth quarter earnings on Wednesday, and investors will watch out for the packaged food maker’s sales volume amid the tough economic climate.Wall Street [expects](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/KHC/earnings/estimates?period=quarterly "expects") the Chicago-based company to post EPS of $0.78, while revenue is expected to decline 3.3% to $6.67 billion during the quarter.The packaged food maker’s U.S. measured retail sales, according to Piper Sandler's research note, fell 3.2% in the fourth quarter compared to consensus of 2.9%. KHC's measured European data also slowed as discussions with French retailers were disruptive and were earlier than usual.Piper Sandler analyst Michael Lavery argued that improvement is still likely two-three quarters away in KHC's U.S. retail momentum.Looking forward, analysts will also try to measure the impact of a potential appointment of Robert Kennedy, Jr., an advocate for healthy eating, and for the eradication of harmful food coloring and additives, as the head of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services.Mizuho downgraded Kraft Heinz to Neutral from Buy mainly due to weakening fundamentals and more challenging outlook, attributing them to declining volume for many food categories despite moderating inflation. The firm reduced its price target for the stock to $31 from $38 previously.Seeking Alpha's Quant Rating and Seeking Alpha analysts have rated the company as a [Hold](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/KHC/ratings/quant-ratings "Hold"), while the Wall Street analysts recommended the stock as a [Buy](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/KHC/ratings/sell-side-ratings "Buy").

Over the last two years, KHC [has beaten EPS estimates](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/KHC/earnings/eps-surprise-summary) 100% of the time and has beaten revenue estimates 25% of the time.Over the last three months, EPS estimates have seen zero [upward revisions](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/KHC/earnings/revisions) versus seven downward moves. Revenue estimates have seen zero upward revisions and 8 downward estimates.

Kraft Heinz's shares have [fallen](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/KHC/charting "fallen") over 20% in the last one year, underperforming the broader S&P500 market, which gained over 20% during the same time.
