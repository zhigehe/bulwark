*Update: The revised post reflects market movements as of 6.45am ET.*

London ([UKX](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/UKX "FTSE 100 Index")) +0.03% staying near record levels.

Germany ([DAX:IND](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/DAX:IND "DAX Germany Index")) +0.24%

France ([CAC:IND](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/CAC:IND "CAC 40 France Index")) +0.02%

The annual inflation rate in the Czech Republic edged down to 2.8% in January.

Portugal's consumer price inflation rate fell to 2.5% in January.

Belgium’s industrial production decreased by 3.8% Y/Y in December.

The pan-European [Stoxx 600](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/STOXX) ([STOXX](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/STOXX "STOXX Europe 600 Index")) traded 0.04% higher after retreating from its record high of 548 Food and beverage stocks traded in green, while oil and gas stocks fell.

Companies slated to release their latest results include Deutsche Boerse, EssilorLuxottica, Michelin, Randstad, and Siemens Energy. Heineken reported an 8.3% increase in full-year operating profit.

**Coming up in the session:** U.S. inflation data chalked for the day. No major events lined up for Europe.

In the bond market, the U.S. 10-year Treasury yield unchanged at 4.54%.

Germany's 10-year yield was up 2 basis point to 2.45%.

UK's 10-year yield was up 1 basis point to 4.52%.
