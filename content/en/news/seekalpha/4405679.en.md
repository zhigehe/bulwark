Information technology (NYSEARCA:[XLK](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/XLK "The Technology Select Sector SPDR® Fund ETF")), ([VGT](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/VGT "Vanguard Information Technology ETF")) saw the largest net buying in the U.S. since December 2021, and the second largest in the past five years, this past week.

According to Goldman Sachs Prime data, the sector was the most net bought in the U.S. through the week starting Jan. 31 and ending Feb. 6, driven by both short covers and long buys in a ratio of 1.5 to 1.

The sector was also net bough every single day this week, Goldman analysts said, and all technology subsectors were net bough, led in notional terms by software ([IGPT](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/IGPT "Invesco Dynamic Software ETF")), ([XSW](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/XSW "SPDR Series Trust - SPDR S&P Software & Services ETF")), semiconductors ([SMH](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SMH "VanEck Semiconductor ETF")), ([SOXX](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SOXX "iShares Semiconductor ETF")), and semi equipment, and “to a lesser extent” information technology services ([FSCSX](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/FSCSX "Fidelity Select Software and IT Services Portfolio No Load")).

Within Goldman Sachs’s Prime book, information technology (NYSEARCA:[XLK](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/XLK "The Technology Select Sector SPDR® Fund ETF")) is now the second-most net bought U.S. sector, after health care ([XLV](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/XLV "Health Care Select Sector SPDR® Fund ETF")).

In addition, the U.S. information technology long/short ratio stands at 1.91, compared to 1.75 at the start of 2025, which is in the 61st percentile, compared to the past year; and in the 21st percentile vs. the past five years.

Technology’s gross exposures, as a percentage of total U.S. book by Goldman Sachs Prime, is now at 17.7%, which are in the 15th percentiles compared to the past year and the 36th percentiles compared to the past five years.
