* Quantum computing stocks were in the spotlight on Thursday as D-Wave Quantum Computing (NYSE:[QBTS](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/QBTS "D-Wave Quantum Inc.")) announced a system sale to Germany's Jülich Supercomputing Centre, making it the first high-performance computing center in the world to own a D-Wave Advantage annealing quantum computing system.
* “The D-Wave Advantage system has played a pioneering role in the development of quantum optimization, both through its contribution to breakthrough research and the development of enterprise-grade solutions across numerous industries,” said Prof. Thomas Lippert, director of JSC, in a statement. “D-Wave stands alone as the only quantum vendor solving real-world problems today, and we’re thrilled to be the first HPC center in Europe to own its groundbreaking annealing quantum computer.”
* D-Wave Quantum Computing shares rose 5% in early trading on the back of the news, while other quantum computing stocks also ticker higher on Thursday, including Quantum Computing (NASDAQ:[QUBT](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/QUBT "Quantum Computing Inc.")), Rigetti Computing ([RGTI](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/RGTI "Rigetti Computing, Inc.")) and IonQ ([IONQ](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/IONQ "IonQ, Inc.")).
