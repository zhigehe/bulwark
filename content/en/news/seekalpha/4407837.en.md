Transocean (NYSE:[RIG](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/RIG "Transocean Ltd.")) -1.3% in early trading Thursday after reporting one new contract and one extension for two of its rigs, raising its contract backlog by $175M, according to its [quarterly fleet status report](https://seekingalpha.com/pr/20000827-transocean-ltd-provides-quarterly-fleet-status-report "quarterly fleet status report").

Transocean (NYSE:[RIG](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/RIG "Transocean Ltd.")) said customer Reliance Industries exercsied a four-well option in India for its ultra-deepwater drillship *Dhirubhai Deepwater KG1* at a $410K dayrate, Equinor exercised a three-well option in Norway for the *Transocean Enabler* at a $428K dayrate, and Woodside Energy agreed to a one-well option in Australia for the *Transocean Endurance* at a $390K dayrate.

Currently chartered to India's ONGC through February 2026, the *Dhirubhai Deepwater KG1* will shift to work for Reliance in April 2026 through May 2029, the *Transocean Enabler* is now signed with Equinor through mid-2027, and the *Transocean Endurance* will work for Woodside through mid-2026.

Transocean's ([RIG](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/RIG "Transocean Ltd.")) total backlog is ~$8.3B as of February 12.
