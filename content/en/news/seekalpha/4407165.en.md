OppFi (NYSE:[OPFI](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/OPFI "OppFi Inc.")) shares dipped 4.9% in Wednesday midafternoon trading, adding to the prior trading session's retreat, after JMP analyst David Scharf downgraded the consumer finance company to Market Perform from Market Outperform on the basis of its valuation.

The stock has [surged more than 300%](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/OPFI/charting "surged more than 300%") in the past year, having reached an all-time high of $17.73 per share on Friday.

"Aside from raised exposure on some retail investor-oriented forums, we are not aware of any incremental fundamental driver of the appreciation," Scharf wrote in a note to clients.

Although he has been optimistic about the company's advancement's in credit and expense management, and anticipated a stronger origination outlook for 2025, the analyst believes the stock is fairly valued at current prices.

What's more, "we also believe that the valuation hurdle has been raised by an additional modest factor as SPAC-era warrants should raise the diluted share count 4%-5% given the current share price," the note said.

Seeking Alpha's Quant system gives OPFI an [A+ valuation grade](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/OPFI/valuation/metrics "A+ valuation grade"), with the best marks in PEG GAAP, price/cash flow and price/sales.
