U.S. consumer borrowing swelled by **$40.85B** in December, compared with the $17.70B increase expected and the $5.37B decrease in the prior month (revised from -$7.50B).

Total outstanding consumer credit of $5.15T climbed from $5.11B in November, which was revised from $5.10B.

On a seasonally adjusted basis, consumer borrowing rose at an annual rate of 2.4%, the Fed [said](https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/g19/current/default.htm "said"), with revolving credit advancing at a 4.8% annual rate and nonrevolving credit drifting up at a 1.6% annual rate.
