S&P Global (NYSE:[SPGI](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SPGI "S&P Global Inc.")) stock climbed 3.8% in premarket Tuesday trading after the company posted better-than-expected Q4 results, exceeding the high end of guidance for revenue and adjusted EPS, and introduced strong 2025 guidance.

The credit-ratings, market index, and data firm expects  [2025 adjusted EPS](https://seekingalpha.com/filing/9533370 " 2025 adjusted EPS ") of $17.00-$17.25 vs. the average analyst estimate of $16.92. Guidance for revenue growth is 5.0%-7.0% vs. the Visible Alpha estimate of 6%.

Q4 adjusted EPS of $3.77, topping the average analyst estimate of $3.47, fell from $3.89 in Q3 and climbed from $3.13 in Q4 2023.

Revenue of $3.59B, beating the $3.50B consensus, increased from $3.58B in the prior quarter and $3.15B a year ago. Revenue from its Ratings and Indices segments led the Y/Y growth, rising 27% and 21%, respectively.

"We reported revenue growth in all segments, as well as strong operating margin expansion and EPS growth for the company, exceeding our consolidated financial guidance on both a GAAP and adjusted basis," said President and CEO Martina Cheung.

Expenses of $2.33B grew from $2.17B in the previous quarter and $2.26B a year ago.

S&P Global's (NYSE:[SPGI](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/SPGI "S&P Global Inc.")) adjusted operating profit of $1.68B vs. $1.74B in Q3 and $1.39B in Q4 2023.

Q4 revenue by segment:

* Market Intelligence — $1.19B rose from $1.16B in Q3 and $1.13B in Q4 2023.
* Ratings — $1.06B vs. $1.11B in the previous quarter and $838M a year ago.
* Commodity Insights — $545M grew from $522M in Q3 and $497M in Q4 2023.
* Mobility — $411M vs. $412M in the prior quarter and $377M a year ago.
* Indices — $436M increased from $416M in the previous quarter and $360M a year earlier.
