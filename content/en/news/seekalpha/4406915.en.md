Denny's Corporation (NASDAQ:[DENN](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/DENN "Denny's Corporation")) fell sharply in Wednesday trading after the restaurant company warned that it has seen a recent slowdown in business over the last few weeks. The concerning January traffic trend led Denny's (NASDAQ:[DENN](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/DENN "Denny's Corporation")) to set Q1 guidance below expectations.

Denny's ([DENN](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/DENN "Denny's Corporation")) earnings call: "When we entered 2025, there was a feeling of the consumer stabilizing and a sense of normalcy ahead. This was even in the face of horrific wildfires and snow storms spanning across the U.S. and even into the Deep South. Yet, there has been a shift in consumer sentiment and a slowing that persisted through the remainder of January and has seemingly accelerated in the last few weeks given the evolving macro environment."

Looking ahead to the full year, Denny's ([DENN](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/DENN "Denny's Corporation")) management said that there is still work to be done within the company's brands, particularly due to near-term consumer sentiment being affected by macroeconomic factors.In terms of strategy, Denny's ([DENN](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/DENN "Denny's Corporation")) plans to accelerate the pace of closures of underperforming locations.

Shares of Denny's ([DENN](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/DENN "Denny's Corporation")) fell 18.2% in Wednesday morning trading. Other restaurant stocks falling in sympathy included Bloomin' Brands ([BLMN](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/BLMN "Bloomin' Brands, Inc.")) -4.8%, Dine Brands Global ([DIN](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/DIN "Dine Brands Global, Inc.")) -3.8%, Cracker Barrel Old Country Store ([CBRL](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/CBRL "Cracker Barrel Old Country Store, Inc.")) -2.9%, and BJ's Restaurants ([BJRI](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/BJRI "BJ's Restaurants, Inc.")) -2.6%
