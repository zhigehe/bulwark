India's Apollo Hospitals Enterprise reported third-quarter profit that beat estimates on Monday, driven by higher demand for its healthcare services.
 
The hospital-chain operator's consolidated net profit rose 51.8 per cent year-on-year to Rs 372 crore ($42.5 million) for the quarter ended December 31.
 
Analysts, on average, expected Rs 351 crore, according to data compiled by LSEG.
