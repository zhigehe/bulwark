French-American economist **Esther Duflo**, who shared the 2019 Nobel Prize for Economics with Abhijit Banerjee and Michael Kraemer, was in Kozhikode recently to speak at the Kerala Literature Festival. Her latest book, *Poor Economics for Kids*(Juggernaut), illustrated by French illustrator Cheyenne Olivier, revolves around the lives of children and adults living in a village that “could be in India or Kenya or Vietnam or elsewhere”, and is based on the research presented in *Poor Economics: Rethinking Poverty and the Ways to End It* (2011), which Duflo co-wrote for adults with Banerjee (who is also her husband).
