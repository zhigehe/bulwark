**On a quarterly basis, net profit rose 41.0%.**

Bharti Airtel, which is 29% owned by [Singtel](https://sbr.com.sg/building-engineering/news/singtels-nxera-dct-bags-643m-green-loan-new-data-centre-in-tuas), reported a 121.3% year-on-year (YoY) growth in net profit to $86m (₹5.5b) in Q3 2025 ended 31 December 2024.

On a quarterly basis, net profit rose 41.0% from $60.3m (₹3.9b) in Q2 2025.

All of its businesses reported revenue growth, with its mobile business revenue increasing 21.4% YoY due to the residual impact of tariff flow-through and an improved portfolio.

Meanwhile, revenue from its homes business grew 18.7% YoY, driven by strong customer additions.

In addition, Airtel Business revenue rose 8.7% YoY despite pressures on wholesale data and messaging, whilst its Digital TV segment revenue posted a 1% increase, with a customer base of 15.8 million.
