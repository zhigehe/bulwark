U.S. futures for the S&P 500 were down 0.1% and futures for the Dow Jones Industrial Average were flat. Changes in futures do not necessarily predict movements after the opening bell.

In Europe, the Stoxx Europe 600 climbed 0.5% in morning trading. Adyen rose 12.1% and Legrand rose 7.5%. On the other hand, Neste slipped 10%, and British American Tobacco fell 7.3%. The FTSE 100 lost 0.4%. Other stocks in Europe were up as France's CAC 40 increased 0.9% and Germany's DAX rose 1.1%.

The Wall Street Journal Dollar Index dropped 0.2% to 101.97.

In commodities, Brent crude fell 0.7% to $74.67 a barrel, and WTI crude fell 0.8% to $70.83 a barrel. The European benchmark price for natural gas, the Dutch futures contract TTF, was down 5.6% to 52.54 euros a megawatt hour.

The German 10-year Bund yield fell by 1 basis point to 2.471% from 2.481%, and the yield on the 10-year U.S. Treasury declined by 3 basis points to 4.605% from 4.629%. Bond prices and yields move in opposite directions.

Stocks in Asia were mixed as Japan's Nikkei 225 index gained 1.3%, whereas Hong Kong's Hang Seng declined 0.2%. China's benchmark Shanghai Composite declined 0.4%.
