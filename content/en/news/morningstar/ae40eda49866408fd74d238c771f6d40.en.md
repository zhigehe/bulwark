Shares of power producers fell after Treasury yields spiked in the wake of inflation data.

"Combined with last week's strong labor report ... the data give the Fed no reason to even think about cutting rates at the moment," said Brian Rose, senior U.S. economist at money manager UBS Global Wealth Management. The strategist noted that Fed Chairman Jerome Powell's description of policy as restrictive implies the central bank still intends to cut rates at some stage.
