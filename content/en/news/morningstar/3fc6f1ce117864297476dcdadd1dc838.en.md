U.S. futures for the S&P 500 were up 0.1% and futures for the Dow Jones Industrial Average were flat. Changes in futures do not necessarily predict movements after the opening bell.

In Europe, the Stoxx Europe 600 declined 0.1% in morning trading. Tomra Systems rose 9.3% and Huhtamaki climbed 6%. On the other hand, TietoEVRY slipped 8.3%, and Fresenius Medical Care lost 5%. The FTSE 100 lost 0.3%. Other stocks in Europe were mixed as France's CAC 40 increased 0.1% and Germany's DAX fell 0.3%.

The Wall Street Journal Dollar Index dropped 0.1% to 101.26.

In commodities, Brent crude climbed 0.5% to $75.42 a barrel, and WTI crude gained 0.5% to $71.64 a barrel. The European benchmark price for natural gas, the Dutch futures contract TTF, was down 3.8% to 49.43 euros a megawatt hour.

The German 10-year Bund yield rose by 2 basis points to 2.442% from 2.423%, and the yield on the 10-year U.S. Treasury rose by 1 basis point to 4.543% from 4.536%. Bond prices and yields move in opposite directions.

Stocks in Asia were mixed as Japan's Nikkei 225 index dropped 0.8%, whereas Hong Kong's Hang Seng gained 3.7%. China's benchmark Shanghai Composite gained 0.4%.
