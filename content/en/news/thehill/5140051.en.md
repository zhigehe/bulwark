**It‚Äôs bears vs. bulls in House budget debate**

Spurred on by Senate GOP plans to release their own, two-bill budget plan this week, Speaker **Mike Johnson** (R-La.) said Tuesday he has had ‚Äúvery productive‚Äù conversations this week about his new fiscal blueprint.

But skepticism is running high among House Republicans that they can advance a framework by the end of the week. Johnson‚Äôs plan, which would encompass a massive party-line tax, border and energy bill, would increase spending cuts but also leave less room for sweeping tax policies. House Budget Committee Chair **Jodey Arrington** (R-Texas) announced his panel will meet tomorrow to mark up the draft.

With key members still squabbling about the numbers to put in the budget resolution, [The Hill‚Äôs Emily Brooks and Mychael Schnell report](https://thehill.com/homenews/house/5139601-house-republicans-doubt-budget-resolution/) there is broad skepticism that the committee will actually advance a House bill.
