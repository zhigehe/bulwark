The Reserve Bank of India’s (RBI) Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) has opted for a “gradual progress” path for the economy rather than a “jerky ride”, amidst growing concerns about overall growth losing momentum. Thus, for the first time in 5 years, RBI has cut the repo rate by 25 bps, from 6.50 per cent to 6.25 per cent, hoping to lift the GDP growth for FY26 fiscal to around 6.7 per cent.
