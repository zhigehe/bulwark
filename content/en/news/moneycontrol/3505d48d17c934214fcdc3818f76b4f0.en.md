Nippon Life India AMC and Nissay Asset Management Japan has announced the launch of the first ever feeder fund in GIFT City, allowing Japanese investors to participate in India's growth story. In a release, the AMC said that this is being launched in partnership with Nissay Asset Management Japan.

The newly introduced “Nippon India ETF Nifty 50 BeES GIFT” is a feeder fund that will invest in Nippon India ETF Nifty 50 BeES.  The ETF tracks India's top 50 companies by market capitalization. According to the AMC, the initiative allows Japanese investors to access India’s rapidly growing stock market and capitalize on the nation’s economic growth. The Nippon Indian ETF Nifty 50 BeEs was launched in 2001. The current one year return of the ETF is around 9.92 percent 3 year return is around 12 percent.

Nissay Asset Management Japan has launched the Nissay India Equity Fund, which will feed into the Nippon India ETF Nifty 50 BeES GIFT Fund. According to the AMC, this move aligns with NAM India’s broader strategy of fostering Japanese investments in India, adding to the four India-focused funds already advised by the firm in Japan and three alternative investment funds (AIFs) managed in India for Japanese investors.

The release also noted that GIFT City which offers favourable tax and regulatory incentives, makes it an ideal location for this Fund. "The Nissay India Equity Fund stands out for its use of GIFT City, a special economic zone in India that provides exclusive tax incentives. The Fund will benefit from reduced taxes on profits from Indian-listed ETFs, reducing operational costs and offering a more efficient investment solution for clients," it added.
